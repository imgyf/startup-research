# Intake Brief — AI Customer Finder

**Phase:** Phase 1 — Intake
**Project:** ai-customer-finder
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Confidence:** Medium (founder context captured; market claims not yet researched)

---

## The Idea (as stated by founder)

An **AI-powered customer finder tool** that "helps businesses find their customers regardless of industry." Form factor open — could be an AI agent. Founder explicitly asked for (a) a recommendation on a lucrative industry to niche down into, if any, and (b) an honest verdict on whether this is worth building.

**Restated problem:** Businesses (esp. SMBs and B2B sellers) struggle to identify and reach the right potential customers. They currently rely on manual prospecting, expensive databases, agencies, ads, and gut feel.

**Restated solution (initial):** An AI agent that, given a description of what a business sells, finds matching prospective customers — and possibly enriches, prioritizes, and/or contacts them.

## The Founder

- **Background:** Technical founder, AI/ML. Can build the product solo. **No specific sales / GTM / vertical domain edge.**
- **Team:** Solo (assumed).
- **Time:** Side project — nights & weekends (~10–15 hrs/week).
- **Budget:** Limited (bootstrapped; treat as <$10k of discretionary spend over 6 months).
- **Founder-market fit (preliminary):** **Moderate–Weak.** Strong on "can build it"; weak on "understands the buyer / can sell it / has a distribution edge." This is the single biggest structural risk and shapes every recommendation below.

## The Market (founder's view)

- Target customer: undefined ("regardless of industry") — **this is a red flag, see Flags**.
- Existing alternatives: not specified by founder. (Reality: a very large, well-funded category — see Phase 3.)
- Known competitors: founder did not name any. (Reality: Apollo, ZoomInfo, Clay, 11x, Artisan, Instantly, Smartlead, Outreach/Salesloft, Unify, AiSDR, and dozens more — see Phase 3.)
- Geography: not specified — assume US/English-speaking first.

## The Business

- Monetization: not specified — assume SaaS subscription (the category norm), possibly usage-based credits for enrichment/sends.
- Pricing: not specified.
- 6/12/36-month goals: not specified — for a nights/weekends bootstrapper, a realistic frame is "≥$1–10k MRR in 12 months, decide whether to go full-time."
- Biggest unknowns (founder's): which vertical to pick; whether it's worth building at all.

## Constraints & Preferences

- **Form factor:** Open to recommendation (autonomous outbound agent vs. prospect/signal-list tool vs. embedded copilot).
- **Vertical:** Fully open — wants the wedge with the best evidence of demand + willingness to pay.
- **Build constraint:** Must be buildable and maintainable by one technical person on limited hours — rules out anything requiring large data-licensing deals, field sales, or heavy ops.
- **Regulatory:** Cold-outreach / data products touch CAN-SPAM, GDPR, CASL, email-deliverability rules, and (increasingly) state privacy laws and platform anti-scraping enforcement. Material, must be addressed (Phase 3 A3).

## Hard Questions — Preliminary Answers

- **Why are you the right person?** Honest answer so far: *you can build it cheaply and quickly*. That is not, by itself, an unfair advantage in a category where building is no longer the bottleneck — distribution, data, and deliverability are. **Needs a sharper answer or a niche where "technical + fast" actually is the edge.**
- **If a well-funded competitor launched this tomorrow?** They already did — many times over, with $50M–$1.6B behind them. Competing horizontally is not viable. The only defensible move is to go where they *won't* go: a narrow vertical or workflow.
- **Strongest argument against:** The category is arguably the single most crowded, most hyped, and most capital-intensive corner of B2B software in 2024–2026, AND the core promise ("AI finds and emails your customers") has a known credibility problem (deliverability collapse, spam complaints, the 11x churn/inflated-metrics episode). A solo nights/weekends entrant with no GTM edge is structurally disadvantaged on every axis except speed-to-ship.
- **Talked to customers?** No (assumed). This must happen in Week 1 of the action plan regardless of the verdict.
- **What would make you walk away?** To be defined in `06-validation/kill-criteria.md`. Candidate: can't get 5 target buyers to say they'd pay within 4 weeks of outreach.

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- **"Regardless of industry" = boiling the ocean + solution-looking-for-a-problem.** A horizontal "find any business its customers" tool competes head-on with Apollo / ZoomInfo / Clay and a dozen funded AI-SDR startups. As stated, this is not a buildable wedge for a solo bootstrapper. The idea must be narrowed to survive.
- **Category saturation + hype-cycle risk.** AI SDR/BDR is among the most crowded and most-funded B2B software categories right now; several flagship startups have had public credibility blow-ups (inflated metrics, churn). New entrants face skepticism, not green field.
- **Founder-market fit is "can build it," not "can sell it."** In this category, building is the cheap part. No GTM/data/distribution edge yet identified.

**Yellow Flags:**
- **Deliverability / compliance headwinds.** Cold email is getting harder (Google/Yahoo 2024 sender rules, spam filtering, more state privacy law). A product whose core value is "we email your prospects" is building on shifting sand.
- **Side-project bandwidth vs. a category that moves weekly.** Funded competitors ship fast; 10–15 hrs/week is tight for keeping pace even in a niche.
- **No named target customer or pricing yet** — everything downstream is assumption until validated.
