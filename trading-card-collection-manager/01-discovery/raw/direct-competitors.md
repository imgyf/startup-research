# Direct Competitor Analysis — Pokemon TCG Collection Manager

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Scope:** US Pokemon collector market (~2.4–2.9M active, ~200K paying-eligible serious hobbyists; SAM $10–40M ARR per Wave 1).
**Entrant profile:** Solo / side-project pace, freemium $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr, camera-scan + portfolio + alerts.
**Method:** 11 web searches + targeted page fetches. Every claim labeled [Data] (sourced), [Estimate] (triangulated), or [Assumption] (unverified).

---

## 1. Competitor Profiles

### 1.1 Collectr (consumer.gg / getcollectr.com)
- **Founded / HQ / Team:** Founded ~2021 [Estimate, based on iOS app ID 1603892248 first listed 2022]. Bootstrapped in Toronto / North America [Data — BetaKit, "How Collectr bootstrapped a trading card hobby into an eight-figure business"].
- **Funding:** Bootstrapped to "eight-figure business" [Data — BetaKit]. Reportedly **declined a16z** [Data — Wave 1 context]. ~4M users [Data — Wave 1 context]; getcollectr.com claims "2M+ users" [Data — getcollectr.com, retrieved 2026-05-10].
- **Pricing (verified 2026-05-10):**
  - Free: 25+ TCGs, unlimited portfolios, marketplace, social, scanning (count not specified). [Data — getcollectr.com/pro]
  - **PRO: $4.99/mo billed annually ($59.88/yr) OR $7.99/mo month-to-month** [Data — getcollectr.com/pro, fetched 2026-05-10].
  - PRO unlocks: unlimited scanning, in-depth pricing data, saved search history, exports, exclusive filters, priority support.
- **Core features:** Multi-TCG scan + portfolio + price tracking + Collectr Social + marketplace + showcase. **No price alerts surfaced** on PRO page [Data].
- **Target customer:** Casual-to-mid TCG collectors across Pokemon/MTG/Yugioh/sports.
- **App Store ratings:** Android **4.80 / 5 stars, ~32K ratings** [Data — Google Play, 2026]. iOS rating not surfaced in search; [Estimate] 4.7+ given Android baseline.
- **Strengths:** (1) Clean UI / best-in-class consumer mobile UX [Data — Reddit consensus]; (2) Massive user base (2–4M) and network effects via social/marketplace [Data]; (3) Multi-TCG breadth.
- **Weaknesses:** (1) **Pricing data accuracy criticized** — "less precise" market tracking, free version limits frustrate users [Data — Reddit/Facebook reviews]; (2) Scanner faulty for Yugioh and Japanese promos, only supports PSA grading (no BGS/CGC) [Data — Facebook group, JustUseApp]; (3) No granular price alerts. **Founder consensus on TikTok: "paid version isn't worth the cost"** [Data — TikTok review].
- **Threat level: HIGH** — market leader by users and brand recognition.
- **Key vulnerability:** Pricing depth + alerting + Pokemon-specific catalog completeness (Japanese, sealed, non-PSA grades). Their breadth is also their shallowness.

### 1.2 CollX (collx.app)
- **Founded / HQ / Team:** Founded 2021, Philadelphia metro [Data — Vista Today, Feb 2025]. ~25–50 employees [Estimate].
- **Funding:** **$10M Series A March 2025** (co-led by Brand Foundry Ventures + 114 Ventures; participants Next Coast, FJ Labs, Ben Franklin Tech Partners, Bobby Witt Jr.). Cumulative >$15M raised [Data — TechCrunch 2025-03-07, cllct.com]. **18 months old as of writing — fresh.**
- **Pricing:** **CollX Pro $9.99/mo or $99.99/yr** ($10/mo or $100/yr); free tier capped at 500 items [Data — CollX FAQ + Grokipedia, 2025]. Pro adds $10 marketplace credit/mo, unlimited items, bulk pricing, exports, AI chatbot (OpenAI).
- **Core features:** Sports-card-first scan + portfolio + **integrated marketplace** + AI assistant. Pokemon supported but secondary.
- **Target customer:** Sports card collectors primarily; secondary TCG.
- **App Store ratings:** Mixed [Data — Trustpilot has critical reviews on customer service, scanner inconsistency, seller protection failures]. Specific star count not surfaced; [Estimate] iOS ~4.5, Android ~4.0 based on review tone.
- **Strengths:** (1) Funded runway + marketplace integration (revenue diversification); (2) Clean UI for beginners [Data]; (3) AI feature differentiation.
- **Weaknesses:** (1) **Sports-card-first DNA — "if your collection includes Pokémon, you'll need a separate solution"** [Data — competitor comparison content]; (2) Marketplace operational issues — sellers losing disputes, missing payments [Data — Trustpilot]; (3) Scanner inconsistency reported [Data].
- **Threat level: MEDIUM** for Pokemon-specific entrants — they're distracted by sports + marketplace ops.
- **Key vulnerability:** Pokemon is a step-child in their roadmap. A Pokemon-native experience can leapfrog without competing on marketplace.

### 1.3 Card Ladder (cardladder.com — Collectors/PSA-owned)
- **Founded / HQ / Team:** Launched June 2020. **Acquired Dec 2021 by Collectors Universe (PSA's parent)** [Data — PSAcard.com article #10556, Sports Collectors Daily]. NOT a $50M raise (Wave 1 correction confirmed).
- **Funding:** Subsidiary of Collectors (private equity-backed, Steve Cohen / D1 Capital). [Data]
- **Pricing:** **PRO $20/mo or $200/yr (raised Feb 1, 2025 from $15/$150)** [Data — onlygreats.com Card Ladder Review, 2024; price-increase coverage]. 7-day free trial.
- **Core features:** Sports-first analytics platform; comprehensive valuations across 18,000+ cards from 15 sources, deep historical sales data (~2 decades), grading population reports. Pokemon coverage exists but indexes are sports-heavy.
- **Target customer:** Investor-tier sports/Pokemon collectors with $5K+ portfolios.
- **App Store ratings:** Niche; iOS app live since 2020 but small review base [Estimate].
- **Strengths:** (1) **Best-in-class historical analytics + PSA pop-report integration** [Data]; (2) PSA brand trust + data moat; (3) Pricing power ($240/yr supports premium positioning).
- **Weaknesses:** (1) **Expensive ($200/yr ≈ 5x entrant price)** — prices out 95% of hobbyists; (2) **No camera scan workflow surfaced** — manual entry friendly to power users only; (3) Sports-first culture, weak mobile UX.
- **Threat level: LOW–MEDIUM** for casual-Pokemon entrant — different price tier and audience.
- **Key vulnerability:** Their $200/yr ceiling creates a wide-open mid-market. They cannot serve the $40-$60/yr collector profitably.

### 1.4 Pokellector (pokellector.com)
- **Founded / HQ / Team:** Long-standing — iOS app live since **2013** (App Store ID 600580227, available 2013) [Data]. Indie developer (Makazu Co per Amazon listing) [Data].
- **Funding:** None disclosed; bootstrapped [Estimate].
- **Pricing:** Free for first 150 cards [Data — Wave 1 + corroborated]. Premium **~$60/yr** [Data — user-cited; quarterly + annual subscription tiers exist] [⚠ flag: subscription confirmed, exact tier table not on website].
- **Core features:** Pokemon-only catalog (English + Japanese sets), scanner, set checklists, web/iOS/Android. Heavy on completionist set-tracking.
- **Target customer:** Pokemon-only completionists / set collectors.
- **App Store ratings:** [Estimate] 4.0–4.5 iOS based on review tone — "great app, sleek and easy" but "ads annoying" + "laggy" [Data — user reviews].
- **Strengths:** (1) **Pokemon-native set checklist** — strongest catalog among incumbents, including Japanese; (2) 13-year brand recognition in Pokemon community; (3) Low price (~$5/mo).
- **Weaknesses:** (1) **Aging app** — laggy, ad-supported on free tier [Data]; (2) Weak portfolio analytics + price trend; (3) No marketplace, weak modern UX.
- **Threat level: MEDIUM** — direct head-to-head Pokemon-specific competitor.
- **Key vulnerability:** UX debt. A modern-feeling Pokemon-first app can displace it. They've been coasting since the 2010s and lost mind-share to Collectr.

### 1.5 Shiny / PokeScope (Pokemon-specific newer entrants)
- **Founded:** 2024 [Data — App Store IDs 6593663349 (Shiny) and 6739146586 (PokeScope) registered 2024-2025].
- **Funding:** Undisclosed; appears solo/small team [Estimate].
- **Pricing:** Shiny — free + lifetime in-app purchase [Data — review mentions "purchasing the lifetime version"]. PokeScope — freemium with subscription tiers [Data].
- **Core features:** Both: scan + portfolio + **price alerts (custom)** + eBay/TCGplayer price aggregation. **These are the closest direct analogs to the entrant's product spec.**
- **Target customer:** Pokemon-only collectors.
- **App Store ratings:** [Estimate] 4.5+ iOS based on positive review content; small review volumes.
- **Strengths:** (1) **Already shipping the alerts + scan + portfolio combo** the entrant proposes; (2) Pokemon-native; (3) Simple UI.
- **Weaknesses:** (1) Small team, slow feature velocity; (2) PokeScope reports camera bugs ("scanner not working on second card"); (3) No social/marketplace network effects.
- **Threat level: HIGH** — these are the entrant's *exact* doppelgangers in 2024–25.
- **Key vulnerability:** They lack scale and brand. An entrant with better execution + targeted distribution can leapfrog. But the entrant must be honest: "another Shiny clone" is not differentiated.

### 1.6 TCGplayer App (eBay-owned, tcgplayer.com)
- **Founded / HQ / Team:** TCGplayer founded 2008, **acquired by eBay 2022** for ~$295M [Data — public]. Hundreds of employees.
- **Funding:** Subsidiary of eBay (NASDAQ: EBAY).
- **Pricing:** **Free.** Monetized via marketplace fees on the eBay-adjacent TCGplayer.com.
- **Core features:** Scan + collection lists + market price (TCGplayer marketplace) + buy/sell. Multi-TCG (MTG/Pokemon/Yugioh/Lorcana/etc.).
- **Target customer:** Marketplace buyers/sellers using TCGplayer.com.
- **App Store ratings:** Mixed; "scanner accuracy / search / UI" complaints; "gradually improving since overhaul" [Data].
- **Strengths:** (1) **Free + eBay-funded distribution moat**; (2) Marketplace integration (where the money flows); (3) Multi-TCG.
- **Weaknesses:** (1) Pricing reflects TCGplayer-marketplace only (not eBay sold comps); (2) Mediocre UX for collection management (commerce-first not collector-first); (3) No price alerts surfaced.
- **Threat level: MEDIUM–HIGH** — free + giant + everywhere. But shape of product is wrong for portfolio tracking, which is the wedge.
- **Key vulnerability:** They're a *commerce* app pretending to be a *collection* app. Collectors don't trust their pricing because TCGplayer prices are seller-listed not sold-comps.

### 1.8 eBay App (ebay.com)
- **Founded / HQ / Team:** Public company, NASDAQ:EBAY. ~33K employees.
- **Funding:** Public.
- **Pricing:** Free.
- **Core features:** **Trading Cards Collection + Price Guide launched 2022–2023** for Pokemon/MTG/Yugioh/sports [Data — eBayinc.com newsroom]. Real-time portfolio valuation. Saved searches with notifications (existing eBay feature) function as de-facto price alerts.
- **Target customer:** Anyone buying/selling on eBay.
- **App Store ratings:** Public marketplace app — millions of reviews, ~4.6+ baseline.
- **Strengths:** (1) **Sold-comp data is the gold standard** (vs TCGplayer ask prices); (2) Free + universal distribution; (3) Saved-search alerts work today.
- **Weaknesses:** (1) **No camera-scan workflow** for collection entry; (2) Collection UX is buried inside a commerce app; (3) Adding 500-card collection manually is brutal; (4) No multi-source price aggregation.
- **Threat level: MEDIUM** — substitutes for the alerts feature, not for portfolio mgmt.
- **Key vulnerability:** Manual-entry friction. The whole reason scanner apps exist.

### 1.9 ManaBox (MTG benchmark — manabox.app)

- **Funding:** Bootstrapped indie [Estimate].
- **Pricing:** **$2.49/mo or $22.99/yr** [Data — App Store, 2026]. Free tier limits 5 decks.

- **Target customer:** MTG collector/player.
- **App Store ratings:** [Estimate] 4.6+ iOS; "best price coverage" + "well-organized intuitive" [Data].
- **Strengths:** (1) **Deepest single-game integration** — proves a one-TCG focused app wins on depth; (2) **Aggressive pricing $23/yr** demonstrates a niche-TCG indie can be profitable at low price; (3) Multi-store price aggregation.
- **Weaknesses:** (1) Recent scanner regression on updates [Data]; (2) White-background scan limitation; (3) MTG-only.
- **Implication for entrant:** ManaBox is the **role model**. A Pokemon-equivalent ManaBox doesn't exist. Pokellector is the closest but UX-aged. **This is the wedge.**

## 3. Positioning Whitespace (Founder's Wedge)

**No competitor does ALL of the following well in Pokemon:**

1. **Sold-comps pricing (eBay last-90-days) + multi-source aggregation** — Collectr/Pokellector use TCGplayer ask; eBay app has the sold data but lacks scan UX. Bridge it.
2. **Modern UX × Pokemon-native catalog depth (incl. Japanese sets, sealed, non-PSA grades)** — Pokellector has catalog but old UX; Collectr has UX but PSA-only and Japanese gaps. Combine.
3. **Genuine custom price alerts on portfolio holdings** — Shiny/PokeScope ship this but with execution issues; Collectr (the leader) doesn't surface it on PRO. Make alerts the headline feature.
4. **Honest, transparent pricing with clean export** — Collectr's PRO is widely reviewed as "not worth it"; trust gap is real. A "no-bullshit" indie with clear value can win on word-of-mouth.
5. **Sub-$5/mo on annual = reachable to mid-tier hobbyist** — Card Ladder $20/mo and Collectr-PRO month-to-month $7.99 leave $3.33/mo (entrant's $39.99/yr) unoccupied. ManaBox proves $2–4/mo works in a niche TCG.

**The wedge:** *"The ManaBox of Pokemon"* — single-game, depth-first, modern, alerts-native, sold-comp-priced, $39.99/yr.

## 4. Vulnerability Ranking (Most → Least Vulnerable)

1. **Pokellector** — aging UX, ad-supported, lost mind-share. **Easiest displacement target** for a modern Pokemon-native app.
2. **Ludex** — Pokemon catalog gaps + scan accuracy complaints + sports-first focus. Easy to outgun on Pokemon depth.
3. **CollX** — distracted by sports + marketplace ops failures. Pokemon is secondary; users will switch for Pokemon-first product.
4. **Shiny / PokeScope** — direct analogs but small teams with bug reports. Vulnerable to better execution + brand.
5. **Card Ladder** — protected by price tier ($240/yr) + PSA brand. Different audience; not directly displaceable but irrelevant to mid-market.
6. **TCGplayer App** — protected by free + eBay backing, but stuck as commerce-first. Not vulnerable but also not the wedge competitor.
7. **eBay App** — too big to displace; substitute risk only on alerts feature. Co-exist by integrating eBay sold-comp data.
8. **Collectr** — most defensible (4M users, brand, social moat). **Hardest target.** Don't fight head-on; flank via Pokemon-depth + alerts + honest pricing.

---

## 5. Strategic Recommendation (Entrant — Solo, Side-Project Pace)

**Position as "the Pokemon-native ManaBox" — single-TCG depth, modern mobile, alerts-first, $39.99/yr honest pricing.** Do NOT compete with Collectr on multi-TCG breadth or social/marketplace network effects; you cannot win that war as a side-project. Instead, displace **Pokellector** (aging) and **Shiny/PokeScope** (under-executed) by combining Pokellector's catalog completeness (English + Japanese + sealed + non-PSA grades) with a Shiny-grade alerts engine and a modern UX, priced beneath Collectr-PRO. Wedge messaging: *"Real Pokemon prices from eBay sold comps, not TCGplayer asks. Custom alerts on every card you own. Built by a Pokemon collector, not a sports-card platform."* Distribution: Reddit r/PokemonTCG, YouTube collector creators, TikTok #pokemontcg — targeting the "Collectr PRO isn't worth it" sentiment that's already in the wild.
