# Brainstorm

**Phase:** 2 — Brainstorm
**Project:** award-flight-alerts
**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Confidence:** Medium

---

## Original Idea (Variation A — "Award Alert App")

A mobile app that notifies frequent flyers when award seats become available on their saved routes/dates/cabins, across multiple airline programs.

**Exciting:**
- Mobile + push is a real UX win over web-first incumbents.
- Clear, narrow value prop ("get the seat before someone else does").

**Risky / Hard:**
- Data acquisition is the entire game. Incumbents (Seats.aero, point.me) have spent years building scraping rigs against active anti-bot defenses.
- Mobile is a feature, not a moat — any incumbent can copy.
- Acquisition cost is high; SEO is dominated by The Points Guy, OMAAT, Thrifty Traveler.

**Competitive change:** Forces incumbents to ship better mobile apps. Probably a 12–18 month head-start at most.

---

## Variation B — "Concierge in Your Pocket"

Not just alerts. The app handles the **entire booking flow** for the user: it finds the award, walks the user through the call-to-the-airline-or-partner-program transfer, automates points transfers from the right credit-card portal, and books on behalf of the user (where possible) or scripts the call.

**Exciting:**
- Removes the most painful part of the redemption (knowing *which* program to transfer to and *when*).
- Higher willingness to pay — saves hours of forum-reading per booking.
- Becomes a habit-forming product, not just a notification.

**Risky / Hard:**
- "Booking on behalf of the user" requires either credentialed access to airline accounts (huge security/legal exposure) or a fee-based human concierge model.
- Concierge model is a service business — already exists (point.me's "Concierge", AwardCat, FlyDealFare, GiltEdge) and is hard to scale.
- Likely needs a small ops team from day 1.

**Competitive change:** Moves from "search tool" to "managed service," a smaller but stickier and more defensible market. Pricing power: $50–250/booking or $50–100/month subscription is realistic.

---

## Variation C — "Flexible-Date Saver"

Pivot away from "I want this exact route on this exact date" toward **"I have N points, surprise me with the best 5 award trips I could take in the next 90 days."** Inspires trips instead of fulfilling pre-decided ones.

**Exciting:**
- Far larger TAM. The "exact route + exact date" customer is a small power-user niche; the "I have points, what should I do?" customer is every cardholder.
- Better viral / share mechanics ("look at this $4 + 25K-mile trip to Tokyo I just found").
- Aligns with how Hopper and Going.com (formerly Scott's Cheap Flights) acquired millions of users.

**Risky / Hard:**
- Going.com / Thrifty Traveler / Dollar Flight Club already do the cash-fare version of this and own the email channel.
- Award availability is intermittent and noisy — quality of "surprise me" output is the entire product, and it's hard.
- Mid-market user has ~50K points, which doesn't unlock the aspirational redemptions that drive virality. **[Yellow Flag]** Most viral redemptions need 200K+ points, meaning power-user audience overlap with Variation A.

**Competitive change:** Competes more with Hopper / Going / Skyscanner than with Seats.aero. Different positioning, broader funnel.

## Convergence — What Resonates

| Element | Variation A | Variation B | Variation C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core insight (mobile + push) | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Differentiation from Seats.aero / point.me | Weak | Strong | Strong (different market) |
| Operational complexity | Medium | High | Medium |
| TAM | Narrow | Narrow but high-value | Broad |
| Defensibility | Low | Medium-High (service moat) | Low (Going.com analogue) |
| Capital needed | Low | Medium-High | Medium |
| Founder fit (assumed builder) | Match | Mismatch (services biz) | Match |
| Acquisition difficulty | Hard | Hard but justified by ARPU | Very hard |

## Refined Idea — Recommended Default

**Lead with Variation A**, but with a credible roadmap to **Variation B** as the long-term moat, and treat **Variation C** as the customer-acquisition wedge (free-tier / inspiration content).

**Concretely:**
1. **MVP (months 0–6):** Variation A — push-notification app for award-availability alerts, with one or two clearly-better-than-incumbent features (e.g., true mobile-native UX, multi-program-in-one-search, transfer-partner suggestions inline).
2. **Wedge (months 0–12):** Variation C as the free / SEO surface — "best award trips this week" weekly roundup, shareable.
3. **Moat (year 2+):** Variation B — concierge / done-for-you booking for the top decile of users.

This staging matches the typical points-and-miles user lifecycle (curiosity → tooling → done-for-you).

**[Opinion]** Going straight to Variation A and competing on "we have a mobile app" is the weakest of the three plays — and is what the founder's one-line idea describes. The skill's job is to push toward a stronger version.

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- None new at this stage.

**Yellow Flags:**
- All three variations share the same data-acquisition problem. The brainstorm does not solve it.
- Variation A on its own is structurally weak vs. incumbents (Seats.aero, point.me, ExpertFlyer).
