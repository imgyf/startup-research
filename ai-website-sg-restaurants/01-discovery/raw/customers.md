# Customer Voice — SG Restaurant Owners & Website Buying Behavior

_Research compiled May 2026. Labels: **[Data]** = sourced figure, **[Estimate]** = inferred/rough, **[Assumption]** = working hypothesis, **[Opinion]** = analyst view._

---

## 1. Current online presence — Instagram-only vs. websites

**[Data] Search-first behavior is the load-bearing fact.** "78% of diners search for a restaurant online before visiting or ordering, with local searches via mobile comprising 63% of restaurant website visits." (Embarque / restaurant-SEO write-up, 2025). For SG, Google + Google Maps are the default discovery surface — Instagram is a brand layer on top, not a substitute.

**[Data] Many SG independent F&B run a thin or stale web presence.** "Many restaurants, especially smaller mom-and-pops, have sites that look like they've been largely ignored: broken links, outdated information, hard-to-read fonts and sites that are not optimized for smartphones." (The Prep, 2025). Consistent with what is observable on Google Maps results for Singapore neighborhood eateries — listing-only, Facebook page, or Instagram bio link.

**[Data] Hawker / very-small operators face a real digital gap.** "Many elderly, independent hawkers lack the skills or the capital to create an online presence for their business, and to compete for visibility on crowded, aesthetically-oriented digital platforms." (Cambridge Public Health Nutrition study; ASEAN Briefing, 2026).

**[Data] But the sector overall is digitizing fast.** IMDA: "95.1% of SMEs have adopted at least one of the six digital areas measured" and in 2024 "97% of SMEs adopted at least one sector-specific digital solution, up from 85% in 2023." Food Services Industry Digital Plan was refreshed July 2025. (IMDA Singapore Digital Economy Report FY24/25, https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/factsheets/2025/ar-sgde-2025).

**[Opinion] What this means for the wedge:** Instagram-only is not "owners are anti-website" — it is "no one bothered to make one cheap and fast enough." The pain is latent, not vocal. Few SG restaurant owners are loudly complaining on Reddit/HWZ that they need a website; they are quietly losing reservations to whoever ranks better in Google. The cold outreach has to **show them** the loss (poor Google ranking, no menu in SERP) rather than ask them about it.

**[Data Gap]** No surfaced verbatim Reddit/HWZ quotes from SG restaurant owners specifically saying "I wish I had a website." This is itself a signal — the market is unconsciously incompetent, not consciously frustrated.

## 2. Cost sensitivity — what SG SMB owners pay for websites

**[Data] Singapore agency benchmark pricing (2025-2026):**
- "Website design in Singapore typically costs between S$2,500 and S$25,000+." (Alf Design Group, 2026)
- "Hiring a website design firm in Singapore starts from SGD 2,500 and can stretch to SGD 50,000+. For most companies building a small business website, expect to pay approximately SGD 6,000, plus an annual SGD 1,000 website design fee." (GoDaddy Asia, 2026)
- Freelancer hourly rate: "SGD 25 to SGD 200." (GoDaddy Asia)
- SEO retainers: "S$500–$5,000+ monthly" generally, "$1,500–$6,000/month for restaurants." (Redot Global; Thrive Agency, 2025)

**[Opinion] SGD 500–1,500 one-time is materially below the market floor.** The cheapest agency quote referenced is S$2,500. A one-time S$500–1,500 price slots below freelancer-agency tier and above DIY (Wix/Squarespace ~S$15-50/mo). This is a real wedge — but also signals "cheap" to some owners, who may worry about quality. Pricing communication must address that.

**[Data] Government co-funding shifts the math.** Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) supports up to 50% (some programs 80–90%) of pre-scoped digital solutions; Enterprise Singapore launched new F&B-cost-management initiatives in Oct 2025 (https://www.enterprisesg.gov.sg/resources/media-centre/media-releases/2025/october/mr04025_enterprise-singapore-introduces-three-new-initiatives-to-help-fb-companies-manage-costs-and-accelerate-digital-adoption). **[Opinion]** If the agency becomes a PSG-listed pre-scoped vendor, effective price to owner drops to S$250–750 — and the funding badge raises perceived legitimacy.

**[Data] HardwareZone SMB cost sentiment.** HWZ threads about running a business in SG focus heavily on recurring monthly cost stack (bookkeeping, corp sec, broadband, taxes). Forum tone is cost-rationalist; price-sensitive but not anti-spend if ROI is shown (HWZ "Actually Singapore quite easy to do own business with the exception of F&B?" thread).

**[Assumption]** Acceptable SG F&B owner ceiling for a one-time website with no recurring lock-in: **S$1,000–S$1,500**. Above S$2,000 they want to see an agency name + portfolio; below S$500 they assume it's a scam or template-spam.

## 3. Who runs digital presence today?

**[Data]** "In France, 80% of restaurants are independent owners, very low-tech and not inclined to work on online marketing." (Kima Ventures, vertical SaaS write-up). **[Assumption]** SG independent F&B mirrors this — likely 60–75% of single-outlet operators have no dedicated marketing headcount.

**[Estimate]** Operator persona splits (working hypothesis pending validation):
- **Owner-operator does it themselves on phone** — ~50–60%. Posts to IG when remembered. No formal SEO/website.
- **Family member (spouse, adult child) handles socials** — ~20–25%. Mid-quality output, no website.
- **Hired part-time marketing/social freelancer** — ~10–15%. Usually a single channel (IG).
- **Agency-managed** — ~5–10%. Almost entirely chains, hotel restaurants, or premium concept restaurants.

**[Opinion]** The buyer is almost always the owner directly. There is no "marketing manager" gatekeeper to bypass for the SGD 500-1,500 segment. That helps walk-ins / direct outreach.

**[Data Gap]** Need primary research (5-10 owner interviews) to confirm the persona split — none of the sources directly broke this down for SG.

## 4. The "show vs. tell" cold-outreach lift

**[Data] This is the strongest piece of evidence in the whole research.** "A study found that when a pitch included a specific diagnosis of a restaurant's online visibility compared to competitors, the conversion rate was 40%, compared to a 10% rate when the pitch was simply 'Hey, I'd like to talk about my product.'" — DataLane "The Canonical Guide to Selling to Restaurants" (https://www.datalane.com/guides/the-canonical-guide-to-selling-to-restaurants).

**[Data]** "Personalized outreach based on restaurant intelligence led to 4x higher response rates." (FirstSales restaurant prospecting guide)

**[Data]** "Personalization beyond first name increases reply rates by 340%" on LinkedIn. (Belkins 2025 study)

**[Opinion] This is the entire premise of the wedge.** A pre-built sample site = "personalized diagnosis + done thing they can react to" turned up to 11. Industry data says the lift over generic cold pitch is roughly **3–4x**. Applied to a 3% baseline cold email reply rate, that's ~10-12% reply, which is the threshold where ~SGD 500-1,500 ACV economics start to work for a 2-person team (see distribution.md).

**[Assumption]** Sample site must be delivered as a fully-rendered preview link, not a screenshot. Owners react to "click and see your restaurant" not "look at this mockup."

**[Risk]** Pre-built sites have a cost-per-prospect. At the AI-native cost structure (Claude / Cursor / generative tooling), assume **S$2–8 of compute + scrape per pre-built site**. Volume matters; see distribution doc.

## 5. What restaurant owners actually want

**[Data] Operator priorities when evaluating tech (2025):** "reliability (37%), ease of use (33%), price/affordability (32%)." (FSR Magazine 2025)

**[Data] Tech spend priorities:** "marketing software (30%), reservation software (30%), staff scheduling (26%)." (FSR Magazine 2025)

**[Data] Diner-side demand (drives owner demand):**
- "78% of diners search for a restaurant online before visiting" (Embarque)
- "Nearly half of restaurant reservations are made online; 59% of diners prefer online reservations" (Tableo 2025)
- "63% of guests prioritize online ordering convenience" (FSR / Tableo)
- Mobile is dominant — "63% of restaurant website visits" via mobile (Embarque)

**[Opinion] Feature priority for the MVP wedge** (ranked):
1. **Google ranking + menu in SERP / Google Business Profile sync** — this is the actual job. Owners care about being findable.
2. **Mobile-first menu page that loads in <2s** — the single most-clicked thing on any restaurant site.
3. **Reservation link** (Chope / Oddle / Sevenrooms embed — don't rebuild reservations)
4. **Photos that don't suck** — owner-supplied phone photos auto-color-corrected
5. **Address + hours + WhatsApp/call CTA**
6. **"Order online" link** to existing delivery platforms

**[Assumption]** Online ordering itself (own checkout) is OUT of scope at this ACV. Punt to GrabFood / foodpanda / Oddle links.

## 6. PDPA & cold outreach legality (SG, 2025-2026)

**[Data] B2B cold email is broadly legal in SG.** "Cold email is legal in Singapore under the PDPA and Spam Control Act if you meet consent and content requirements, with B2B cold emails allowed with business relevance." "Messages targeting businesses (i.e. B2B) and not individuals are exempt from certain DNC Registry requirements." (Reachoutly; ASEAN Briefing; iSmart Communications, 2025-2026).

**[Data] Requirements that still apply:**
- Subject line must not be misleading
- Must include valid unsubscribe / opt-out
- Must include sender's physical/business identity
- DNC Registry rules apply to **individual** Singapore phone numbers (8/9-prefix mobile, 6-prefix residential) for SMS/calls — NOT to generic restaurant `info@`, `hello@`, or business landlines used for business comms.
- Spam Control Act (SCA) governs bulk commercial messages.

**[Data] Penalties for PDPA breaches:** up to **SGD 1 million per violation**, or **10% of annual SG turnover** for orgs over SGD 10M turnover. (PDPC; HeySara 2026 guide). DPO appointment + notification to PDPC mandatory since 1 Jun 2025.

**[Opinion]** For cold-emailing publicly listed restaurant `info@` / `reservations@` addresses with a business-relevant pitch + opt-out + identity, exposure is **low** if (a) volume is reasonable (sub-1000/day per sender), (b) bounce rate <2%, (c) one-click unsubscribe honored, (d) no scraping of personal Gmail addresses of staff. **Walking up to the restaurant in person is not PDPA-regulated at all** — that's just a sales conversation.

**[Data Gap]** No specific PDPC enforcement action found targeting cold B2B email senders in 2025-2026 — enforcement focus has been on data breaches, NRIC misuse, and unauthorized telemarketing to consumers, not B2B email outreach.

## Categorized themes

### Pain points (largely latent)
- "I don't know where to start" — paralysis, not resistance
- Site is stale, content drift, can't update menu
- Bad Google ranking, beaten by aggregator sites
- Phone photos look bad on a website
- Don't know how to handle reservations through site

### Objections you'll hear on cold outreach
- "Already on Instagram / Facebook / Google Maps — why do I need this?"
- "I tried Wix / Squarespace, it was too complicated"
- "How much is the monthly fee?" (assumption: there must be one)
- "Will you help me update it after?"
- "Is this PSG eligible?"
- "Can it take orders?" (manage expectations — say no, link out)

### Language owners use
- "Google" not "SEO" — "I want to come up in Google"
- "Menu" not "PDF" — they want to upload a photo of the menu
- "Reservations" not "booking flow"
- "Promote" not "marketing"
- "Cheaper" + "no hidden cost" — recurring obsession

### Decision criteria (inferred priority)
1. **Trust** — does this person look real? (Singapore base, name, face, portfolio)
2. **Price + no recurring lock-in** (or PSG-funded)
3. **Speed** — "how fast can it go live"
4. **Shown sample of their own restaurant** (the wedge)
5. **Updates after launch** — who maintains it
6. **Owns the domain** — surprisingly common worry post-Wix-trauma
