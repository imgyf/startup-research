# US Med Spa Market Sizing — Refined (raw)

Research date: 2026-05-11. Pivot context: 2-person team weighing a US med-spa website agency in DFW or Tampa/Orlando (vs. their original SG-restaurants plan). This file refines TAM, ownership, marketing spend, formation rate, channels, and regulatory wrinkles.

Source tiers: **T1** primary (AmSpa, gov, vendor data pages). **T2** trade press / market-research summaries / consulting whitepapers. **T3** SEO blogs, scraper directories.

---

## 1. How many med spas — US, TX, FL, AZ, metros

**[Data]** AmSpa "State of the Industry": **~8,899 US med spa locations (2022) → 10,488 (2023) → forecast ~11,553 by 2025**; industry revenue >$17B, growing >$1B/yr; >100,000 employees; "more than 11,000 med spas." Source: americanmedspa.org/blog/amspas-2024-medical-spa-state-of-the-industry-report..., americanmedspa.org/resources/med-spa-statistics (T1, 2024 report w/ 2023 data — **no 2025 report yet; AmSpa publishes ~every 2 years**).

**[Data — but DOUBT]** RentechDigital scraper: "**52,833 medical spas in the US** as of 2026-04-01; **Texas 7,306; Florida 7,737**; Florida cities — Miami 3,112, Orlando 827, Tampa 389." Source: rentechdigital.com/smartscraper (T3, 2026). **[Opinion]** This count is ~5x AmSpa's clinical-med-spa figure — it almost certainly sweeps in day spas, salons, esthetician studios, IV-drip bars, weight-loss clinics, and Google-Maps category mislabels. **Treat as an outer bound, not a real count.** A defensible "real clinical med spa" estimate: TX and FL each have **roughly 1,000–1,800 clinical med spas** (TX+FL+CA+AZ+NY are the top states; TX/FL each likely 10–15% of the ~11,500 US total). **[Estimate]** Metro splits (clinical): DFW ~250–450; Tampa Bay ~150–300; Orlando ~150–300; Scottsdale/Phoenix ~200–350. Low confidence — derived, not sourced.

**[Data]** Directory sources you could mine for actual counts: RentechDigital, ByteScraper ("34k+ med spas USA"), guidetoflorida.com, Yelp/Google Maps, AmSpa member directory, state cosmetic-laser/medical-board registries. Source: bytescraper.com, guidetoflorida.com (T3, 2025-2026).

## 2. Revenue, margins, ownership profile

**[Data]** Average annual revenue per med spa (2023, AmSpa): **~$1,398,833** (~$117k–$122k/mo for a single location); high performers and multi-site >$2M. Source: joinblvd.com/blog/average-medical-spa-revenue citing AmSpa 2024 report (T2, 2025).
**[Data]** Profitability: med spas are generally profitable; widely cited margins ~15–30% mature, lower in year 1–2 (startup CAPEX ~$300k–$600k). Source: zenoti.com/thecheckin/are-med-spas-profitable, financialmodelslab.com (T2/T3, 2026).

**[Data]** **Ownership / structure**:
- **~81% of US med spas are single-location** owner-operated. Source: AmSpa 2024 via physiciangrowthpartners.com, joinblvd.com (T2, 2024-2025).
- **NPs have nearly caught up to MDs/surgeons** as the largest owner group (driven by state laws allowing independent NP practice); RNs and aestheticians own a meaningful slice; "physician-owned" is no longer the majority profile. Source: AmSpa 2024 recap, joinblvd.com (T2, 2024-2025).
- **Only ~3% are private-equity-owned (2023)** but **consolidation is accelerating** — >50 announced med spa M&A deals/yr in 2023 & 2024 (vs. a handful in 2019-2020). Active platforms/consolidators: MedSpa Partners (MSP, Persistence Capital — 40+ clinics, $275M continuation fund w/ Morgan Stanley PE), Advanced MedAesthetic Partners (Leon Capital), Skytale-advised deals; chains LaserAway (~200 clinics by late 2025, laser-focused), Milan Laser, SEV Laser, Ideal Image, OVME, OrangeTwist, Inspire Aesthetics. Source: physiciangrowthpartners.com/medical-aesthetics-private-equity, scoperesearch.co, bio2laserstudio.com, americanmedspa.org/blog/private-equity-and-growth-capital... (T2, 2024-2025).

**[Opinion]** **Addressable = the ~81% single-location independents that are NOT yet rolled up and NOT a franchise.** Franchises/chains (Sona, Ideal Image, LaserAway, Milan Laser, SEV Laser, OrangeTwist, etc.) get sites from corporate — exclude entirely. PE roll-up portfolio clinics (MSP, AMP) also centralize web — exclude. Net realistic SAM in a single metro like DFW: maybe **150–350 independent single-location med spas**, of which the warm subset (poor site / new opening / actively unhappy with an agency) is a fraction of that. **This is a small market per metro** — viable for a 2-person lifestyle agency, not for anything VC-scale.

## 3. % of independent med spas with a poor / outdated / no website

**[Data — weak]** No clean survey found. Adjacent signals: AmSpa's "Marketing Investment Gap" piece — roughly **half of med spa practices under-invest in marketing** while the other half pull ahead; SEO-audit vendors claim "most aesthetic sites miss" big chunks of a 47-point checklist. Source: americanmedspa.org/blog/the-marketing-investment-gap..., authorityspecialist.com/.../seo-checklist (T2/T3, 2025-2026).
**[Estimate]** Given ~81% are owner-operated and ~half under-invest in marketing, a reasonable working assumption: **~30–50% of independent single-location med spas have a website that's outdated, template-stock, slow, non-conversion-optimized, or effectively just a one-pager / Google Business Profile.** This is an **[Assumption]**, not data — needs validation by manually auditing 50–100 DFW/Tampa med spa sites.

## 4. Marketing / website spend

**[Data]** Med spa marketing budgets: typically **5–10% of revenue** (range 2–15%); startups/new spas **10–15%**; established (3+ yrs) **8–12%**. For a ~$300k-revenue spa that's **~$2,500/mo**; for the ~$1.4M average that implies **~$7k–$14k/mo** at 6–12%, though many real practices spend far less. Cited monthly figures: "$5,000–$15,000/mo, average ~$6,800/practice," and a more conservative "~$3,900/mo average." 60–80% of the budget should go to digital; "website & conversion optimization" ~10–15% of the marketing budget. Source: americanmedspa.org/blog/industry-experts-weigh-in..., growth99.com/blog/med-spa-marketing-budget-manual, themedspaagency.com/med-spa-marketing-spend, sagapixel.com, medspamastery.co, patientgain.com/med-spas-advertising-spend (T1/T2/T3, 2025-2026).
**[Opinion]** The SG analysis's "~$2,800/mo avg marketing" is **plausibly low for the average med spa** (US med spas are bigger businesses than SG restaurants) — call it **$3,000–$7,000/mo realistic median, with a long right tail to $15k+**. Website builds: 10–15% of a marketing budget on web means a **one-time custom site of ~$4,000–$10,000** is well within norm; boutique/creative-led shops (Influx) push $10k–$20k+. SG's "$4–8k website builds" estimate holds, maybe skews a touch higher.

## 5. New med spa formation rate

**[Data]** Net adds: **+1,589 locations 2022→2023** (8,899→10,488); AmSpa: "17% of all existing med spas were started as recently as 2021." Industry market-size CAGRs cited 14–23% (revenue, not unit count). Source: AmSpa 2024 report, technavio.com, precedenceresearch.com (T1/T2, 2024-2026).
**[Estimate]** Net new locations ≈ **1,500–2,500/yr nationally** in 2024-2025 (gross openings higher, offsetting some closures). **[Estimate]** TX + FL combined ≈ **~250–500 new med spas/yr** (top-2 states, ~15–25% of US openings). New openings = the single best warm-lead source (they have no site, no agency, and Moxie is actively chasing them — so speed matters). Low confidence on the metro-level number.

## 7. Regulatory wrinkles (brief)

**[Data]**
- **Medical director / supervision**: every med spa needs a licensed physician as medical director; corporate-practice-of-medicine rules and NP/RN scope-of-practice vary by state. TX and FL both have specific med spa supervision/delegation rules. (Doesn't affect a website vendor directly, but it's why ownership ≠ who's listed on the site.) Source: yocale.com/blog/med-spa-laws-by-us-states, spencerfane.com, djholtlaw.com (T2, 2025).
- **HIPAA**: applies to any med spa that provides medical treatments / keeps medical records / transmits health info electronically — **regardless of cash-pay**. So contact forms collecting health info, intake embeds, and stored before/after photos are PHI → need BAA-covered hosting/form vendors; a pure brochure site with no health-info capture is largely outside HIPAA scope. Source: patientnow.com/.../med-spa-hipaa-compliance-guide, martilawgroup.com, medspastandards.com/blog/hipaa-compliance-guide (T2, 2025-2026).
- **Before/after photos**: require a **specific, separate written HIPAA authorization** naming the marketing/social use and platforms, with revocation rights — a generic intake consent is insufficient; stored photos must be secured like medical records. Build the gallery with a consent-tracking field. Source: portraitcare.com/post/compliance-101-medical-spa-policies-and-procedures, patientgain.com/hipaa-advertising-medical-spas (T2, 2025-2026).
- **FTC**: testimonials/endorsements must be truthful, reflect actual results, and disclose material connections (free services, payment, influencer relationships); before/after images must not mislead; fines up to ~$43,792/violation. Source: FTC endorsement guides via medmindmarketing.com, portraitcare.com (T2, 2025-2026).
- **Also**: ADA/WCAG accessibility on the site (Growth99 markets "ADA compliance protection" as a feature → it's a real buyer concern / lawsuit vector).
**[Opinion]** None of this kills a website business — but it raises the bar vs. a restaurant site: you need HIPAA-aware hosting/forms, a consent-aware photo gallery, and disclosure-compliant testimonial sections. It's also a *selling point* — most cheap DIY/Wix sites get this wrong.
