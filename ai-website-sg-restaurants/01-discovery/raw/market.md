# Market Data — AI-Native Website Agency for Singapore Restaurants

Research date: 2026-05-11. All currency in SGD unless noted. Source tiers: T1 = official/analyst (SingStat, IMDA, SFA, EnterpriseSG); T2 = tech press / established trade press; T3 = blogs / vendor blogs / social.

Data label legend: **[Data]** verified, **[Estimate]** modelled, **[Assumption]** working assumption, **[Opinion]** subjective.

## 1. F&B / Restaurant Establishment Count in Singapore

- **[Data]** **53,471 licensed food establishments** in Singapore as at end of 2023 — total figure from SFA (covers retail and non-retail; includes food shops, food stalls, food factories, supermarkets, private markets). Source: SFA Singapore Food Statistics 2023 / SingStat ebook. Tier 1. (https://www.sfa.gov.sg/docs/default-source/publication/sg-food-statistics/singapore-food-statistics-2023.pdf, https://www.singstat.gov.sg/publications/reference/ebook/industry/food-establishments). Status: ~18 months old, borderline stale — flag for refresh.
- **[Data]** SingStat F&B Services Index (the operating-restaurant universe, excluding factories/supermarkets) has subcategories: restaurants, fast food, food caterers, cafes/food courts/other. October 2025 total F&B sales ~SGD 1.0B/month. Source: SingStat MRS October 2025 (https://www.singstat.gov.sg/-/media/files/news/mrsoct2025.ashx). Tier 1.
- **[Estimate]** The "addressable restaurant" universe (restaurants + cafes + bars + caterers, excluding hawker stalls, food factories, supermarkets) is commonly cited at **~27,000–30,000 outlets** in SG industry pieces. Founder should verify via SingStat Table Builder query on SSIC 56 (Food and Beverage Service Activities).
- **[Data]** **2,431 F&B outlets closed Jan–Oct 2025** (~307/month), up from ~254/month in 2024 — implies churn rate of roughly 10–12%/year against the operating base. Source: The Independent SG, NewInAsia.com (https://theindependent.sg/report-307-closures-of-food-establishments-a-month-in-2025-due-to-high-costs-fewer-diners/). Tier 2.
- **Gap:** Specific 2024 and 2025 totals not found in search; founder should pull from data.gov.sg "Licensed Food Establishments (End Of Period), Annual" (https://data.gov.sg/datasets/d_a9e81ab29216b10b69e23e7957b680b9/view). Tier 1.

**Confidence: Medium** on universe sizing (53k incl. all license types is real but inflated for our SAM); High on closure rate trend.

## 2. Website vs Social-Only Adoption (SG SMBs / F&B)

- **[Data]** SME digital adoption overall: **95.1% in 2024** (IMDA SG Digital Economy Report 2025), avg 2.3 of 6 digital domains adopted per SME (up from 2.0). Source: IMDA (https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/press-releases/2025/singapore-digital-economy). Tier 1.
- **[Data]** **97% of SMEs adopted ≥1 sector-specific digital solution** in 2024 (up from 85% in 2023). Same IMDA source. Tier 1.
- **[Data]** SME AI adoption tripled 4.2% → **14.5% in 2024**, mostly off-the-shelf GenAI (84%). IMDA. Tier 1.
- **[Data]** Singapore: 5.16M social media identities (88.2% of population), Jan 2025. Instagram 3.19M users (48.9%). **24.7% of Singaporeans follow restaurants/chefs/food on social.** Source: DataReportal Digital 2025 Singapore, Meltwater, Hashmeta. Tier 2.
- **[Estimate]** "% of SG restaurants with no proper website" — **not found as a clean stat.** [Opinion] Anecdotal vibe from F&B agency blogs: a meaningful share of independent cafes/eateries operate Instagram-first, with delivery-platform pages (Foodpanda/Grab) substituting for a website. [Assumption] Working assumption: **40–60% of independent (non-chain) SG F&B outlets lack a self-owned website** — founder should validate via a 50-outlet manual audit before pricing the wedge.
- **[Data]** Online sales = 25.1% of F&B services revenue in Oct 2025 (vs 26.2% Sep). Source: SingStat MRS Oct 2025. Tier 1. — Implies ~1/4 of F&B revenue already flows through digital channels, but largely via delivery aggregators rather than restaurant-owned sites.

**Confidence: High** on overall SME digital adoption (95.1%) — but this metric is broad and conceals that "digital adoption" often means a Facebook page, not a website. **Low** on the specific website-vs-social-only F&B split — biggest data gap.

## 3. Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) for Websites

- **[Data]** **PSG is active and was enhanced at Budget 2026 (effective 1 April 2026)**: support **up to 70% for SMEs** (previously 50%), annual cap **SGD 30,000 per UEN per calendar year**. Source: 2stallions, Grantla, Elite Asia, EnterpriseSG (https://www.enterprisesg.gov.sg/financial-support/productivity-solutions-grant). Tier 1.
- **[Data]** Eligibility: company registered & operating in SG, ≥30% local equity, group turnover ≤ SGD 100M OR ≤ 200 employees. Tier 1.
- **[Data]** PSG **covers websites and e-commerce solutions**, including digital asset creation. Vendors must be **pre-approved and listed on the GoBusiness Gov Assist portal**. Source: EnterpriseSG FAQ, OOm, GoBusiness. Tier 1.
- **[Data]** Named PSG-approved web vendors (non-exhaustive): **Verz Design, Orfeo Story, MediaPlus Digital, OOm, MSWeb International** (sources: their own marketing pages). Tier 2/3 — founder must verify each via GoBusiness portal (https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg/productivity-solutions-grant/all-psg-solutions/) before citing.
- **[Data]** Verz Design also claims **EDG (Enterprise Development Grant) approval — up to 80% support** for qualifying small businesses. Source: smartsinga.com competitor review. Tier 3.

**Implication for our concept:** SGD 1,500 one-time site at our pricing × 50% PSG = **SGD 750 effective cost to the restaurant**, or × 70% post-April-2026 = **SGD 450 effective**. To capture this, we'd need to become a PSG-approved vendor — a non-trivial application process that competitors have already cleared.

**Confidence: High** on PSG mechanics; Medium on which specific vendors are currently listed (changes frequently).

## 4. SG F&B Industry Health 2025–2026

- **[Data]** **307 F&B closures/month in 2025** vs 254/month in 2024 vs ~230/month in 2022–2023. Worst since pandemic. Source: The Independent SG, IDN Financials, NewInAsia (https://theindependent.sg/report-307-closures-of-food-establishments-a-month-in-2025-due-to-high-costs-fewer-diners/, https://newinasia.com/2025/09/04/singapore-fnb-closures-pierre-marechal-insight/). Tier 2.
- **[Data]** **82% of SG F&B businesses never recorded a profit during their entire existence** (annual tax declarations). **>60% don't survive 5 years.** Source: Rubbish Eat Rubbish Grow citing tax data (https://rubbisheatrubbishgrow.com/2025/12/04/why-so-many-fbs-are-closing-down/). Tier 3 (blog) but widely cited; founder should locate primary source.
- **[Data]** Restaurant margins typically **3–8%**, "lucky" ones 5–7%. Source: industry pieces (Urban List, RestaurantTimes). Tier 2/3.
- **[Data]** F&B Sales Index Oct 2025: +2.4% YoY (recovery from -1.6% in Sept). SingStat. Tier 1.
- **[Data]** Closures span all tiers — fine dining, bars, cafes, coffee shops, food courts. Drivers: high rent, food costs, manpower shortage, declining dine-out frequency in 2025. Source: Eatbook, NewInAsia, Urban List. Tier 2.

**Implication:** Customer LTV concern — selling a SGD 1,500 site to a business with ~50% 5-year mortality means a meaningful share of customers won't be around to renew/upsell. But also creates demand: new openings need a website fast and cheap, and stressed operators want low-touch marketing wins.

**Confidence: High** on the contraction narrative; Medium on the specific 82%-never-profitable stat (single source, founder should triangulate).

## 5. Restaurant Tech Spending (SG SMB)

- **[Data]** Recommended digital marketing budget for SG single-outlet restaurants: **SGD 500–2,000/month** (i.e., SGD 6K–24K/year). Source: Hashmeta, Eats365, marketingagency.sg. Tier 2/3.
- **[Opinion]** Most independents underspend the recommendation — agency blogs explicitly state "most restaurateurs have almost zero or very little marketing budget" outside chains. Source: oncoffeemakers.com. Tier 3.
- **[Data]** Delivery platform commissions: **15–35% of order value** on Foodpanda/Deliveroo; FoodPanda raised consumer platform fee to SGD 0.60/order from Jan 2025. Source: Menuviel, Mothership, SingSaver. Tier 2. — These are the "default" digital channels eating into the margin; an owned website is partly an attempt to reduce dependence on them.
- **Gap:** Specific annual website/tooling spend by SG F&B SMBs not found as a clean stat. **Suggest founder verify via Restaurant Association of Singapore (RAS) member surveys.**

**Confidence: Medium** — budget guidance is from agencies trying to sell services, may be inflated.

## 6. EnterpriseSG / IMDA Programs Affecting This Market

- **[Data]** **SMEs Go Digital** is the umbrella IMDA program; PSG sits under it. Source: IMDA (https://www.imda.gov.sg/how-we-can-help/smes-go-digital). Tier 1.
- **[Data]** **Refreshed Food Services Industry Digital Plan (IDP)** announced 2025 at Restaurant Asia / SIGEP Asia. Four pillars: Digital Solutions Roadmap, AI use cases for F&B, Cybersecurity & Data Protection Roadmap, Digital Skills Training Roadmap. Source: IMDA press release 2025 (https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/factsheets/2025/enterprise-singapore-and-imda-announce-refreshed-food-services-industry-digital-plan). Tier 1. — **Very relevant: explicitly calls out AI use cases for F&B.**
- **[Data]** **CTO-as-a-Service** (free advisory for SMEs choosing digital solutions) explicitly applied to F&B. IMDA blog Nov 2024 (https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/blog/blog-articles/2024/11/ctoaas-helps-food-and-beverage-smes-go-digital). Tier 1.
- **[Data]** **EDG (Enterprise Development Grant)** — separate scheme, up to 80% in some cases for upgrading projects. Verz Design markets as EDG-eligible vendor.
- **Gap:** Specific 2026 funding envelopes for F&B digital under SMEs Go Digital not found — founder should call EnterpriseSG directly.

**Confidence: High** on program existence; Medium on whether our pricing fits inside the PSG pre-approved-vendor caps (each PSG-listed solution has a fixed maximum reimbursable amount).

## Cross-References to `competitors.md`

1. **PSG vendors are simultaneously a competitor and a path to market.** Verz Design, OOm, MediaPlus, Orfeo Story (Section 3 above; profiled in competitors.md Part E) — they win on subsidy access. Becoming PSG-approved is a strategic question, not just a regulatory one.
2. **SGD 500–1,500 pricing is *below* the typical "Basic tier" SGD 1,500–5,000 from local agencies** (competitors.md Part C). This positions us as a clear value disruptor — but founder must confirm agencies aren't already discounting deeply on PSG-subsidized deals.
3. **Horizontal AI builders (Wix Harmony, Squarespace Blueprint, Durable)** cost USD 16–25/month (competitors.md Part A) — that's <SGD 400/year. The restaurant could just do it themselves. Our wedge has to be the **done-for-you cold-outreach demo**, not the tooling itself.
4. **Delivery aggregator dependence (15–35% commissions)** = restaurants are paying *thousands per month* in implicit "digital fees" to Foodpanda/Grab. A SGD 1,500 one-time site that captures direct orders could pay back in weeks — strong angle for sales pitch.
5. **307 closures/month** (Section 4) overlaps with competitors.md Part B (Oddle, Chope) — these vendors also feel the customer-churn pain. Oddle's zero-startup-cost model is a response.
