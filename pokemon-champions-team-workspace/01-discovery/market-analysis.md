# Market Analysis

**Phase:** 3 — Discovery
**Project:** pokemon-champions-team-workspace
**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Confidence:** Medium-High (game launch + competitive scene data is strong; specific tool-segment economics inferred from adjacencies)

---

## Executive Summary

The market for a Pokémon Champions prep workspace is **active, addressable, and partially underserved** — but it is **not greenfield**. *Pokémon Champions* shipped April 8, 2026 to mediocre reception (Metacritic 65 / OpenCritic 58) with deliberate gaps that have created tool-shaped vacuums (no replay URLs, 3 free team slots, no version history, $4.99/mo "Membership" for 18 slots). At least **15 Champions-specific companion tools** shipped within 30 days of launch, validating both the demand and the speed of the land grab. **No funded entrant exists yet** — the entire competitive set is solo-founder or hobbyist projects.

The gating insight: the casual segment is huge but doesn't pay. The tournament-prep segment is small but already pays well — WolfeyVGC alone has **10,893 active paid Patrons** (Graphtreon; Tier 2) and Metafy charges **$25-30/hr** for human Pokémon coaching (Tier 2). A workspace targeted at the tournament-prep persona at **$15/mo** is consistent with proven category WTP, not a stretch.

## Market Sizing

### TAM — Top of Funnel

[Data, Tier 2] *Pokémon Scarlet/Violet* sold **26.79M units** through 2025 (TPC quarterly + Famitsu). This is the loosest possible TAM proxy — every owner is a potential Champions buyer.

[Data, Tier 2] *Pokémon Showdown* runs **>1M MAU**, **>30K peak concurrent users**, and **18M visits/month** at **21-min average session** (SimilarWeb + Showdown analytics references). This is a much closer proxy for "people who already use a competitive tool."

[Estimate] Realistic addressable population for a Champions companion tool in the first 18 months: **300K-700K monthly active users** (combining lapsed Showdown users now playing Champions + new Champions-curious players seeking help on ranked).

### SAM — Active Competitive Players

[Data, Tier 1/Tier 2] **VGC Worlds 2024 had 943 competitors**; Regional events drew **270-676 players each** (Pokemon.com event reports; Limitless tournament data). Across an annual VGC season, an estimated **~10K-20K players** compete in sanctioned tournaments globally. This is the "tournament-prep" SAM — small but high-value.

[Estimate] **Competitive-but-unranked** Pokémon ladder players who'd plausibly subscribe to a workspace tool: **50K-150K** based on tools like Pikalytics' visible engagement (no public DAU; inference from creator audiences).

### SOM — Reachable in Year 1

[Estimate] Year 1 realistically reachable population (English-speaking, present in Reddit r/stunfisk + r/PokemonChampions + competitive Twitter + creator audiences) is **20K-40K aware-and-engaged users**. At 1-3% conversion to paid (typical SaaS for self-serve gaming tools), that's **200-1,200 paying users** in Year 1 — consistent with the founder's ambition stated in intake.

## Market Maturity & Growth

**Stage:** Growing. The category of "AI-augmented competitive game companion tools" is in active formation:
- [Data, Tier 1] **Mobalytics (League of Legends companion) was acquired by ESL FACEIT in March 2025** (TechCrunch; Tier 2). Estimated $5-25M ARR pre-acquisition — proves competitive-game prep tools can build venture-scale outcomes in adjacent franchises.
- [Data, Tier 1] **Chess.com** generates **$100M+ revenue** with **~1% paid conversion** to its Premium tier (chess.com 2024 disclosures; Tier 1) — proves that even a "free tools dominate" community supports scaled paid products with the right value layer.
- [Data, Tier 2] op.gg, Blitz.gg, U.GG operate similar models in League and Valorant.

**Growth drivers (Pokémon-specific):**
1. *Champions* is TPC's first standalone competitive product — net new format-creation that doesn't compete with mainline RPG cycles.
2. Switch 2 install base growing through 2026.
3. *Champions* mobile launch later in 2026 will expand the player base further.
4. The persistent failure of Champions to ship native team-management tooling is a sustained tailwind, not a one-time gap.

**Growth headwinds:**
1. Competitive Pokémon culture is **strongly free-tool** (Showdown, Smogon, Pikalytics — none monetize directly).
2. *Champions* itself could ship native upgrades that erase the wedge — but TPC's historical product velocity is slow.
3. The 15+ shipped competitor tools in 30 days indicate that **nominal differentiation is not enough**; positioning has to be sharp.

## Regulatory & IP Posture

**Key finding:** [Data, Tier 2] **The Pokémon Company has never issued C&D against Showdown, Smogon, Pikalytics, or any teambuilder/calculator/companion tool in 15+ years** (cross-referenced from multiple coverage sources; no documented enforcement actions found across the research). TPC enforcement targets:
- ROM hacks and emulator distributions (numerous, e.g. Pokémon Uranium)
- Asset reuse without license (Palworld lawsuit, sprite-using fan games)
- Commercial use of TPC-owned trademarks

Companion tools that **(a)** use no sprites/assets, **(b)** sit on PokéAPI/Showdown public data, and **(c)** don't claim official endorsement appear to operate without friction.

**Standard fan-tool posture is appropriate.** Risks remain non-zero — TPC could change policy, especially if a tool achieves visibility — but the historical baseline supports a "build carefully and don't trademark-infringe" posture.

[Yellow Flag] If the tool monetizes prominently and includes "Pokémon" in the brand name, the risk surface widens. Phase 5 brand work should bias toward neutral naming.

## Timing Assessment

**Verdict: Time-pressured. Not too early, possibly too late if execution drags.**

- **Game has shipped** — the wedge is real, not speculative.
- **Competitor land grab is active** — 15+ tools shipped in 30 days; VGCCoach already owns the "AI coach" SEO position.
- **Tournament calendar creates urgency** — Indianapolis Regionals (May 29-31), NAIC (June 12-14), Worlds (Aug 28-30). Each is a major demand-spike for prep tools.
- **Worlds-aligned product debut window** — a tool that ships meaningful value by ~July gets the late-Worlds-prep audience naturally.

[Yellow Flag] At 15-25 hrs/week, a 3-month MVP timeline is realistic but tight. Anything longer means launching past peak Worlds-prep demand into a quieter mid-season trough (Sept-Oct).

## Strategic Implications for the Startup

1. **Target the tournament-prep persona, not the casual market.** The casual market has near-zero WTP and pulls scope toward feature breadth. The tournament-prep persona has demonstrated WTP via Patreon and Metafy, concentrates in <10 channels, and is already calendar-driven (which makes retention easier).

2. **Price at $12-15/mo standard tier, not $5-8.** WTP benchmarks support it; competitor pricing is mostly free (race to bottom) or absent (premium opportunity). See `01-discovery/raw/wave3-personas.md`.

3. **Win on workflow, not on better data.** Showdown/Pikalytics have unassailable data and SEO moats. Workspace, version history, and AI coaching grounded in @pkmn/sim are unowned territory.

4. **Tournament calendar is the GTM clock.** Build/launch beats are dictated by Regional + International + Worlds schedules — not standard SaaS launch logic.

5. **Creator partnership is the fastest acquisition channel.** WolfeyVGC's audience overlap with the target persona is high; a Patreon-tier-locked benefit + revshare is the most leveraged single deal available.

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- None. The market is real and the WTP signals are concrete.

**Yellow Flags:**
- **Crowded launch field** — 15+ tools already shipped means commodity-tier features (basic teambuilder, basic calc) are no longer differentiating. We must lead with the workflow + coaching wedge from day one.
- **Tournament-calendar dependency** — building toward Worlds-prep demand peak is sound, but it also concentrates risk: a missed July ship slips us to a much weaker Sept-Oct trough.
- **Commodity Pokémon tooling culture** — community norms expect free utilities. We need to be visibly more valuable than Pikalytics + Showdown combined to justify any paid tier.
- **Founder credibility gap** in tournament-prep persona — see `00-intake/brief.md`. Mitigation via creator partnership (Wave 2 GTM) is essential, not optional.
