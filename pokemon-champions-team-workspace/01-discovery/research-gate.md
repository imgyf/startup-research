# Research Gate — Go/No-Go Checkpoint

**Phase:** 3.5 — Research Gate
**Project:** pokemon-champions-team-workspace
**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Verdict:** **YELLOW LIGHT — Conditional GO**

---

## TL;DR for the Founder

This is a **real opportunity** — the market exists, the competitive set is unfunded, and willingness-to-pay is concretely demonstrated by adjacent benchmarks (WolfeyVGC's 10,893 paid Patrons; Mobalytics acquired in 2025; Chess.com $100M+ revenue). The Pokémon Champions launch left specific, exploitable gaps in team management, replay, and coaching.

But this is **not a green light**. The window is narrowing fast (15+ tools shipped in 30 days), the founder's credibility gap matters in a community where top-player association drives trust, and the casual market — which made the original idea attractive — should explicitly NOT be the launch target.

You should proceed to full strategy ONLY IF you accept three pivots from the original framing.

---

## What the Research Found

### Strong Signals (Argue For)

1. **The wedge is real and specific.** Champions ships with ~3 free team slots, no replay URLs, no version history, and no coaching layer. This isn't a hypothetical gap — it's documented behavior in a live game with a 65 Metacritic and active player frustration on Reddit *right now*.

2. **WTP is demonstrated.** WolfeyVGC has 10,893 paid Patrons (added 5,706 in the 30 days post-launch). Brady Smith has 206+ paid Patrons in a single tier. Metafy charges $25-30/hr for human coaching. The community pays — for prep value delivered well.

3. **No funded competitor.** All 15+ Champions-specific tools that shipped in the 30-day land grab are solo-founder or hobbyist projects. No VC plays. The ceiling is open.

4. **Adjacent category validated.** Mobalytics × ESL FACEIT acquisition in March 2025 proves the playbook for AI-powered competitive game companion tools.

5. **Tournament calendar creates demand-spike opportunities.** Indianapolis Regional (May 29-31), NAIC (June 12-14), Worlds (Aug 28-30) — three concrete demand-amplifier events in the next 4 months.

6. **IP risk is moderate-low.** TPC has not C&D'd Showdown, Smogon, Pikalytics, or any companion tool in 15+ years. Standard fan-tool posture is appropriate.

7. **Open-source ingredients dramatically reduce build risk.** @pkmn/sim, @smogon/calc, @pkmn/client are MIT-licensed and proven. PokéLLMon paper validates the grounded-LLM coaching pattern.

### Concerning Signals (Argue Against)

1. **15+ Champions-specific tools shipped in 30 days post-launch.** The land grab is partway through. Late entrants need sharper differentiation than the early movers needed.

2. **VGCCoach already owns the "AI coach for Champions" SEO position.** A direct head-on attack on that keyword is unwinnable. Repositioning is required.

3. **Pikalytics shipped Champions support within 30 days** and could expand into workspace features any time. They have brand authority and SEO. If they ship team versioning before we ship our MVP, our wedge narrows substantially.

4. **Founder credibility gap.** Hobbyist + technical, not a tournament-tier player. Persona C (tournament-prep) will sniff this out fast. **Mitigation requires a competitive-player advisor or co-founder commitment before launch — not after.**

5. **The casual market is a trap.** ~50% of Champions buyers are casuals with near-zero WTP. The original idea framed casuals + competitives in one product. Research argues *against* this configuration — pulled-too-thin product, conflicting UX needs, broken pricing.

6. **TPC could ship native team management** in a content patch within 6-12 months and obsolete a third of our wedge. Mitigation: AI coaching + longitudinal memory + cross-format sync are moats TPC won't replicate quickly.

7. **Free-tool cultural norms.** Showdown/Smogon/Pikalytics are all free. The community pays for content (creator Patreons), but plain SaaS utility faces friction.

---

## What This Means for the Original Idea

The original idea — *one product serving casuals and competitives, persistent workspace, matchup analysis, post-loss improvement* — needs **three pivots** to survive contact with the data:

### Pivot 1: **Tournament-prep persona (C), not casual market (E), as the launch target**
- The original framed casuals as a giant TAM "feeder funnel" to competitive.
- Reality: casuals don't pay, churn fast, and pull product toward vibes.
- Pivot: Build for **VGC Tournament Aspirants** at $15/mo. Casual flow becomes a free-tier inbound funnel only — never paid acquisition.

### Pivot 2: **Manual match journaling, not auto-replay analysis**
- The original framed AI matchup analysis from replays as core.
- Reality: Champions has no replay URLs, no API, no programmatic match data. Auto-analysis is technically infeasible.
- Pivot: **Structured manual journaling** — the user enters key game moments; AI helps reflect. Lower magic, equally durable JTBD.

### Pivot 3: **Pricing 2-3x higher than original hypothesis**
- The original hypothesized $5-8/mo Pro tier.
- Reality: Adjacent benchmarks support $15/mo standard, $25/mo tournament-prep.
- Pivot: Raise pricing to align with Mobalytics ($7.99/mo) + Wolfey Patreon ceiling ($5-10/mo) + Metafy ($25-30/hr) — *capturing the value of "10x cheaper than coaching, 10x more useful than free tools."*

These three pivots cumulatively turn a contested casual-tool market into a **focused tournament-prep workspace** with proven WTP, sharper positioning, and feasible scope.

---

## Verdict — YELLOW LIGHT, Conditional GO

**Proceed to full strategy IF you accept all three pivots.**

If you accept only some, the math degrades:
- Reject Pivot 1 (keep casual focus): WTP is too low for the business model to work.
- Reject Pivot 2 (keep auto-replay): MVP is technically infeasible at the founder's commitment level.
- Reject Pivot 3 (keep $5-8 pricing): Year-1 ARR collapses below subsistence even at high SOM.

**If you accept all three pivots, the opportunity becomes a tractable side-project-to-bootstrap-business with realistic Year 1 ARR of $36K-$90K** (200-500 paying users at $15/mo) and a credible path to $200K+ in Year 2 with Worlds-prep retention and a creator partnership lock-in.

## Critical Risks to Carry Into Phases 4-8

These are the risks the strategy and validation phases must explicitly address:

1. **15+ competitors in market, none dominant** — speed-to-meaningful-MVP matters more than feature completeness.
2. **Founder credibility gap** — advisor recruitment is non-negotiable.
3. **TPC ships native team management** within 6-12 months — moats must be AI coaching, longitudinal memory, cross-format sync.
4. **Pikalytics ships workspace** — monitor monthly; have a defensibility story ready.
5. **WolfeyVGC partnership unvalidated** — alternate creator candidates must be ranked.
6. **Tournament-calendar dependency** — missing the July ship target slips us into a Sept-Nov demand trough.
7. **Casual-market pull** — discipline required to NOT build for the largest TAM.
