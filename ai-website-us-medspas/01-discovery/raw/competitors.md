# US Med Spa Web / Marketing Agency — Competitive Landscape (raw)

Research date: 2026-05-11. Pivot context: 2-person team considering "AI-native website agency for US med spas (DFW or Tampa/Orlando)" using a pre-built spec-site cold-outreach wedge. Question: how saturated is this, who are the players, what do they charge, can the spec-site wedge survive?

Source tiers: **T1** = primary (vendor pricing pages, AmSpa, gov). **T2** = trade press / aggregators / market-research summaries. **T3** = SEO listicles, blog roundups, scraper directories.

---

## 1. Med-spa-specialized web / marketing agencies

**[Data]** **Growth99** (Salt Lake City) — positions as "better than a med spa digital marketing agency"; an integrated platform: custom website design + secure hosting + ADA compliance + unlimited updates ("Accelerator" entry tier), plus SEO, Google/Meta ads, CRM, patient automation. So it's a **marketing-platform + agency hybrid, website is the on-ramp, not the product**. Exact public pricing not disclosed; AmSpa members get 20% off "all plans and setup fees" (implies a setup fee + monthly model). Source: growth99.com/web-design-hosting, /speciality/med-spa, americanmedspa.org/vendor/growth99 (T1/T2, 2025-2026).
**[Estimate]** Growth99 packages likely $500–$2,500/mo with a setup fee in the low thousands — consistent with the AmSpa-discount language and competitor pricing below. (T3-grade inference.)

**[Data]** **PatientGain** — HIPAA-compliant marketing platform; med spa plans **$999–$1,999/mo** (à la carte apps from $499/mo, e.g. "Rewards"). Includes website, SEO, ads, "SPOC" inbox app. Quotes cost-per-lead $16–$27. This is a **marketing retainer with website included**, not a website shop. Source: patientgain.com/best-med-spa-marketing-company, /med-spa-online-marketing-a-advertising (T1, 2025-2026).

**[Data]** **MyAdvice** (formerly Advice Media; absorbed older healthcare-web brands) — "affordable medical spa SEO" + social tooling + AI captions + website. Marketing retainer model; pricing not public. Source: myadvice.com/medical-spa-marketing (T1, 2026).

**[Data]** **Influx Marketing** (Park City, UT) — "digital agency focused on patient acquisition for cash-pay healthcare"; leads with creative production (pro photo/video), custom websites, SEO, PPC, social. Premium creative-led retainer; pricing not public (positioned upmarket). Source: influxmarketing.com/medspa-marketing (T1/T2, 2025-2026).

**[Data]** **Aesthetic Conversion** — "Website and SEO programs" for med spas, conversion-rate framed. Website + SEO bundle (retainer). Pricing not public. Source: aestheticconversion.com (T1, 2025).

**[Data]** **ClinicGrower** — digital marketing for aesthetic practices; claims 400+ practices served; SEO + paid + funnels. Retainer/consulting model, not a website shop. Source: clinicgrower.com/aesthetic (T2, 2025).

**[Data]** **The Aesthetics Junkie** — marketing strategy, client-acquisition "systems," coaching; runs the "Medical Spa Marketing Show" podcast. Coaching/agency hybrid. Source: theaestheticsjunkie.com (T2, 2025).

**[Data]** **Skytale Group** — NOT a web agency: investment banking / M&A / management consulting for medical aesthetics; offers AmSpa members a free strategic assessment. Irrelevant as a web competitor; relevant as evidence the vertical is PE-mature. Source: skytalegroup.com/who-we-serve/medical-aesthetics, americanmedspa.org/vendor/skytale-group (T2, 2025).

**[Data]** **The Med Spa Agency** — publishes med-spa marketing-spend benchmarks; full-service marketing agency (retainer). Source: themedspaagency.com/med-spa-marketing-spend (T2, 2025-2026).

**[Data]** Others repeatedly named in 2025-2026 "best med spa marketing agency" roundups: Pulse Marketing, Sagapixel, Brenton Way, Thrive Agency, MedStar Media, Diamond Accelerator, JustDigital, Comrade, Middleway, Stranded, Aesthetix CRM, MedMind Marketing (also does compliance consulting), Patient Generator, Aesthetic Brand Marketing, Webware.ai, Workee.ai. Source: thriveagency.com, brentonway.com, medstarmedia.com, zioadvertising.com, healthcare.wowbix.com (all T3 listicles, 2025-2026).

**[Opinion]** This is a **crowded, mature vertical**. There are 10+ med-spa-specialized agencies plus a long tail of generalists who have a "med spa" landing page. Almost none sell a website as the product — they sell **monthly marketing retainers ($999–$2,500+/mo)** with a website as the loss-leader/anchor. A pure "we build your website for $4–8k once" play is a *weaker* business than what incumbents run, and incumbents will happily build a site to win a retainer. Two of the biggest (Growth99, PatientGain) are also software platforms — they own the customer relationship via CRM/inbox.

## 2. Med-spa vertical SaaS that bundles a website / booking microsite

**[Data]** **Moxie (joinmoxie.com)** — all-in-one med spa OS aimed at *new* med spa entrepreneurs (launch + run + grow): scheduling, intake, payments, charting, inventory, compliance, **plus "customized website creation with online appointment booking"** and 1:1 coaching. Performance-based pricing, no fixed monthly fee ("we only succeed when you succeed"). Claims "launch 3x faster, 50% cheaper." Source: joinmoxie.com (T1, 2025-2026). **This is the single biggest threat to the wedge**: Moxie literally gives a launching med spa a real website + booking + back office bundled. New med spas (the warmest leads) are Moxie's core market.

**[Data]** **Boulevard** — client-experience platform for medspas/salons; ~$425/mo+; **login-free online booking overlay added directly to the practice's own website** (not a separate microsite). So Boulevard expects you to *have* a website; it doesn't replace it. Source: joinblvd.com, portraitcare.com/post/boulevard-vs-mangomint (T1/T2, 2026).

**[Data]** **Mangomint** — top-rated scheduling/management for med spas; ~$245/mo; same model — booking overlay on *your* site, "clients never get redirected to a site that's not yours." Doesn't provide a website. Source: mangomint.com (T1, 2026).

**[Data]** **Aesthetic Record** — budget med spa EMR/booking; booking pages, not a real website. Source: mangomint.com/go/mangomint-med-spa-software-aesthetic-record (T2, 2026).

**[Data]** **Zenoti** — all-in-one for salons/spas/medspas, ~$400+/mo, enterprise/multi-location lean; booking widgets, not a website builder. Source: zenoti.com/medical-spa-software, softwareadvice.com (T1/T2, 2026).

**[Data]** **Mindbody** — all-in-one wellness/beauty business mgmt (booking, payments, marketing); booking pages + a directory listing, not a real custom site. Source: portraitcare.com/post/alternatives-mindbody (T2, 2026).

**[Data]** **Vagaro** — booking platform; **website builder available as a paid add-on** (also a branded app add-on). So Vagaro *does* offer a (template-grade) website. Source: portraitcare.com/post/alternatives-patientnow, patientnow.com (T1/T2, 2026).

**[Data]** **PatientNow / RxPhoto** — practice-management EMR for aesthetics/plastics/derm; EMR, patient engagement, payments, "digital marketing" services; bundles marketing as a service line (so PatientNow competes on the marketing side too). Source: patientnow.com (T1, 2026).

**[Data]** **Symplast** — plastic-surgery/aesthetics EHR; clinical, not website. Source: portraitcare aggregator (T2, 2026).

**[Data]** **Podium** — not med-spa-specific; reviews/messaging/payments/AI receptionist add-on; no website. Mentioned in med spa stacks. Source: general (T3, 2025-2026).

**[Opinion]** Closer call than restaurants/Oddle but **still concerning**. Most med spa SaaS (Boulevard, Mangomint, Zenoti, Mindbody) deliberately does NOT replace the website — it overlays booking on whatever site you have, which actually *helps* a website vendor. BUT (a) **Moxie bundles a real website for the launch segment** — the warmest leads — and is venture-backed and aggressive, and (b) **Vagaro, Wix, Squarespace, GoHighLevel-based agencies, and PatientNow** all give a "good enough" site. The "good enough free site already exists" problem from the SG/Oddle analysis is *partially* present here via Moxie + DIY builders, less so via the booking SaaS.

## 3. Horizontal AI website builders (DIY floor)

**[Data]** **Wix** — has med spa / wellness templates and an AI site builder; 800+ integrations (incl. booking). **[Data]** **Squarespace** — "most popular platform among boutique med spas," elegant templates, booking integrations; AI builder. **[Data]** **Durable** — AI builder favored for speed/budget over design. **[Data]** **Framer AI**, **Showit** (template marketplaces like MedSpa Marketing Shop, The MedSpa Society sell Showit/Canva med spa templates for ~$100–$500), **ThemeForest** (255 med spa templates). Source: wix.com/blog/how-to-make-a-med-spa-website, durable.com, colorlib.com, medspamarketingshop.com (T1/T3, 2026).
**[Opinion]** The DIY floor is **$0–$50/mo + a $100–$500 template**. A med spa owner's spouse or office manager can stand up a passable Squarespace site in a weekend. This caps what an unsophisticated buyer will pay and is why incumbents bundle the site into a retainer rather than sell it standalone.

## 5. AI app builders for delivery (brief — covered in SG analysis)

**[Data]** Lovable, v0 (Vercel), Bolt.new, Replit Agent, Cursor, Google Stitch dominate "vibe coding" in 2026. Lovable and Base44 have **SOC 2 Type II / ISO 27001 / GDPR**; **Bolt has NO HIPAA/SOC2/GDPR** — rules it out for anything touching PHI. Source: getmocha.com/blog/best-ai-app-builder-2026, dyad.sh, technically.dev (T2/T3, 2026).
**[Opinion]** Med-spa-specific delivery considerations: (a) **HIPAA scope** — a brochure site with no intake/PHI is mostly out of HIPAA scope; the moment you add a contact form that collects health info or embed a booking widget that takes intake, you're handling PHI and need a BAA-covered host/form vendor — pick the builder/host accordingly (Lovable + a HIPAA-compliant form/booking embed; avoid Bolt). (b) **Before/after photo galleries** are table-stakes for med spa sites and require per-photo written HIPAA authorization from the patient — build the gallery as a CMS the owner controls, with a consent-tracking field. (c) **Online booking** is usually NOT something you build — you embed the med spa's Boulevard/Mangomint/Vagaro/Aesthetic Record widget. So delivery cost per site is low (mostly your time + a Lovable/Cursor seat), call it well under $100 of tooling per site for a 2-person team doing 10–20/mo. The constraint is sales, not delivery.

## 6. Is "we built you a sample site" novel here?

**[Data]** Several med-spa-targeted vendors already do **free demos/mockups**: Webware.ai offers "free demo bookings to show how they can build medical spa websites from start to finish"; Workee.ai pitches launching a site for you; Webflow's "Made in Webflow" gallery lets prospects clone med spa templates for free. Template shops (MedSpa Marketing Shop, The MedSpa Society) sell ready-made med spa sites. Source: webware.ai/pages/industry/medspa-website-design, workee.ai/blog/best-medspa-websites, webflow.com/made-in-webflow/medspa (T1/T3, 2026).
**[Opinion]** "We'll show you a demo / free mockup / template preview" is **standard practice** in this space — it is NOT a novel wedge. What *might* still be mildly differentiated: building a *fully personalized, production-ready* site for a *named specific prospect before any contact* (true spec work, not a generic demo) and cold-outreaching with it. That's a labor/effort signal incumbents mostly don't bother with at the cold-outreach stage. But "spec site as cold outreach" is an execution tactic, not a moat — anyone with Lovable can copy it the day they see it work, and agencies that do free mockups are 80% of the way there already.

## Biggest gaps

1. **No public setup-fee/monthly numbers for Growth99, Influx, Aesthetic Conversion, MyAdvice, ClinicGrower** — all quote-only; pricing is estimated.
2. **No data on incumbent churn or how many med spas actually have/keep an agency** vs. DIY — can't size the "switchable" market precisely.
3. **No hard number on how many med spas use Moxie / Boulevard / Mangomint** and therefore already have a bundled or overlay-ready site.
4. **No evidence on whether cold-outreach-with-a-built-spec-site actually converts** in US med spas specifically — only that free demos are common (which suggests low marginal novelty).
5. **GoHighLevel-based "med spa marketing" sub-agencies** are a huge invisible long tail (white-label CRM+site+funnel) not captured by name in roundups — likely the real competitive density.
