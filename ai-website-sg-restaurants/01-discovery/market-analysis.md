**Project:** ai-website-sg-restaurants
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Confidence:** Medium-High (key data points sourced from SFA, IMDA, SingStat, EnterpriseSG; one critical gap on website-vs-social split)

## TAM / SAM / SOM

| Layer | Definition | Size | Source / Method |
|-------|-----------|------|-----------------|
| **TAM** | All licensed food establishments in SG (incl. stalls, factories, supermarkets) | **53,471** outlets (end-2023) | [Data, SFA Singapore Food Statistics 2023, Tier 1] |
| **SAM** | Restaurants, cafes, bars, food caterers (SSIC 56 — outlets that could plausibly own a brand website) | **~27,000–30,000** outlets | [Estimate, industry-cited slice of SSIC 56] |
| **Beachhead SAM** | Independent (non-chain) SG outlets *without* a self-owned website | **~10,800–18,000** outlets | [Estimate, 40–60% of SAM — **biggest data gap**, must validate with 50-outlet manual audit] |
| **SOM (Year 1)** | Outlets reachable + closable by a 2-person team via walk-ins/email | **30–80 customers** | [Estimate, based on ~3-6 walk-in closes/week/founder × 26 working weeks × 70% margin for ramp/no-show] |

**At SGD 1,000 ACV, Year 1 revenue SOM = SGD 30,000–80,000.** That funds a two-person bootstrapped agency at break-even but is not venture-scale.

## Market Health

- **F&B sales growing modestly:** F&B Services Index +2.4% YoY Oct 2025 [Data, SingStat MRS Oct 2025, Tier 1]
- **But operator pain is severe:** 307 outlet closures/month in 2025 (up from 254/month in 2024) — worst since the pandemic [Data, Tier 2]
- **Profitability is rare:** 82% of SG F&B businesses never recorded a profit in their entire existence; >60% don't survive 5 years [Data, blog citing tax data — Tier 3 single source, founder should triangulate]
- **Margins: 3–8% typical** [Data, multiple Tier 2/3 sources]
- **Online F&B revenue share: 25.1%** of total F&B revenue (Oct 2025), mostly via delivery aggregators — not owned channels [Data, SingStat, Tier 1]

## Digital Adoption Picture

- **95.1% SME digital adoption** in 2024 [Data, IMDA SG Digital Economy Report 2025, Tier 1] — but this is broad ("at least one digital area"); many "digitized" restaurants run on Foodpanda + Instagram, not a website
- **SME AI adoption tripled** from 4.2% to 14.5% in 2024 [Data, IMDA, Tier 1] — culture is ready for AI-delivered products
- **24.7% of Singaporeans follow restaurants/chefs on social** [Data, DataReportal Digital 2025 SG, Tier 2] — restaurants compete on visibility, which a good website + SEO can amplify

## Regulatory & Subsidy Landscape — the Decisive Factor

**Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG)** is the strategic fulcrum for this market:

- **70% support** from 1 April 2026 (up from 50%), SGD 30k annual cap per UEN [Data, EnterpriseSG, Budget 2026, Tier 1]
- Restaurant must be SG-registered, ≥30% local equity, ≤SGD 100M turnover OR ≤200 employees
- Covers **websites and e-commerce solutions** — but **only from pre-approved vendors** listed on GoBusiness Gov Assist
- Pre-approved competitors include **Verz Design, OOm, MediaPlus, Orfeo Story, MSWeb International** [Data, vendor marketing pages, Tier 2/3]

**The math problem this creates:**

| Scenario | Price to customer (effective) |
|----------|------------------------------|
| Our SGD 1,500 site, unsubsidized | SGD 1,500 |
| Verz Design SGD 5,000 site at 70% PSG | **SGD 1,500** |
| Verz Design SGD 3,000 site at 70% PSG | **SGD 900** |

**For PSG-eligible restaurants, our price advantage is neutralized — and we lose on trust (no PSG badge, no government endorsement).** This is the single most important strategic constraint.

**Refreshed Food Services Industry Digital Plan (IDP)** — IMDA + EnterpriseSG, July 2025 — explicitly calls out **AI use cases for F&B** as a pillar [Data, IMDA press release, Tier 1]. Positive alignment for AI-native positioning if we can plug into the ecosystem.

## Spend Capacity of Target Customer

- **Recommended digital marketing budget** for SG single-outlet restaurants: SGD 500–2,000/month [Tier 2/3 — vendor-cited, may be inflated]
- **Actual spend** by independents: agency blogs say most underspend the recommendation significantly
- **Delivery aggregator commissions:** 15–35% of order value [Data, Tier 2] — restaurants are *already* paying thousands per month in implicit "digital fees" to FoodPanda/Grab/Deliveroo. **This is the strongest sales angle for an owned website.**

## Timing Assessment

| Force | Direction |
|------|-----------|
| AI delivery cost (Lovable/v0/Bolt) | Falling sharply — favors entry now |
| Wix/Squarespace AI features | Improving fast — favors entry now, before they dominate |
| PSG subsidy generosity | Increasing (70% from April 2026) — *unfavorable* to non-approved vendors |
| F&B operator pressure | Rising closures + margins → more openness to revenue-boosting tools |
| Government push on F&B digitization (IDP refresh) | Active and inclusive of AI |
| New agencies in space | Few "AI-native" agencies targeting SG F&B specifically — modest greenfield |

**Net:** The window is open but **closing**. A 2026 entry is timely; a 2027 entry will face commoditized AI tooling + entrenched PSG-approved competitors.

---

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- **PSG-eligible competitors at 70% subsidy match or beat our list price.** Without PSG approval, we serve only the non-eligible segment (foreign-owned outlets, brand-new outlets without UEN registration history, customers who refuse paperwork) — a much smaller pool than the 27-30k SAM suggests.
- **High customer mortality (60%+ in 5 years, 82% never-profit)** means LTV from a one-time SGD 500-1,500 sale is brittle. Subscription/retainer also at risk if customers go under.

**Yellow Flags:**
- **Website-vs-social-only gap is unverified.** The 40-60% beachhead estimate is the load-bearing number for sizing. **Must be validated** with a 50-outlet manual Google audit before the founder commits significant time.
- **Online F&B revenue already 25%** of sales — but it flows through aggregators, not owned sites. The sales pitch must reframe owned-site value (margin recovery), not just "you need a website."
