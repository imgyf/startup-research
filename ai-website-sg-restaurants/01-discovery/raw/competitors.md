# Competitive Landscape — AI Website Agency for Singapore Restaurants

Research date: 2026-05-11. All pricing in stated currency (USD or SGD). Source tiers: T1 = official/analyst, T2 = tech press / established blogs, T3 = vendor/random blogs.

Data label legend: **[Data]** verified, **[Estimate]** modelled, **[Assumption]** working assumption, **[Opinion]** subjective.

---

## A. Horizontal AI Website Builders

### Wix (Wix AI / Harmony / Aria)
- **[Data]** Pricing: paid plans from **USD 17/month** (~SGD 23). Source: Wix vs Squarespace 2026 comparison (https://www.wix.com/blog/wix-vs-squarespace). Tier 1.
- **[Data]** **Wix Harmony** = prompt-to-site AI builder; **Aria** = canvas-aware AI agent that can generate pages, perform on-canvas actions, refine copy. Tier 2.
- **[Data]** Has restaurant templates + **Restaurant Experiences** add-on (events scheduling, hosting). Tier 2.
- **Strengths vs us:** Mature, brand recognition, ecosystem, restaurant-specific features (ordering, reservations, POS integrations).
- **Weaknesses vs us:** DIY (the restaurant owner has to drive the prompt + content). Generic templates — no SG-specific localisation (no PayNow, no GrabFood/Foodpanda menu sync). Subscription forever vs our one-time.

### Squarespace (Blueprint AI)
- **[Data]** Pricing from **USD 16/month** (~SGD 22). Tier 1 (https://www.squarespace.com/compare/wix).
- **[Data]** Blueprint AI = guided question-flow → assembled site (less conversational than Wix). Tier 2.
- **[Opinion]** Best aesthetic out-of-the-box of all horizontal builders; recommended for boutique brands under 100 products. Tier 2.
- **Weakness vs us:** Same DIY-burden + recurring-cost problem as Wix.

### Durable
- **[Data]** Generates a full site in **~30 seconds**. Launch plan **USD 22/month** (annual). Tier 2 (https://www.squarespace.com/blog/best-ai-website-builder, dyad.sh).
- **[Data]** Bundles invoicing, blog, CRM — positioned as solo-business OS, not just website.
- **Strength vs us:** Fastest "name → site" experience on market — directly competes with our wedge claim. Their speed is the demo.
- **Weakness:** No SG payment rails, no restaurant vertical depth.

### 10Web
- **[Data]** From **USD 10/month**. AI builder + WordPress hosting + speed/image optimisation. Has explicit **Restaurant Website Builder** sub-product (menus, reservations). Tier 2 (https://10web.io/ai-website-builder/restaurant/, https://10web.io/pricing-platform/).
- **Strength:** Restaurant-specific output, WordPress (transferable). Cheapest in this set.
- **Weakness:** Still DIY; no SG integrations.

### Hocoos
- **[Data]** **USD 15/month** (or ~USD 12.50/mo annual). Free with subdomain. Question-flow → AI generation. Tier 2/3.
- **Weakness:** Less brand recognition; thinner restaurant features.

### Mixo
- **[Data]** From **USD 7/month**. Landing-page focused (not full sites). Tier 2/3.
- **Weakness:** Wrong product shape for a restaurant (single landing page, not menus + reservations + location).

### Framer (Framer AI)
- **[Data]** Free tier; paid from **USD 10/month**. Generates multi-page mobile-responsive wireframes in <60s from prompt. Tier 2.
- **Strength:** Best output quality among AI builders for design-conscious customers (cafes, fine dining).
- **Weakness:** Designer-tool DNA — owners can't maintain it easily after handoff.

**[Opinion] Category takeaway:** All horizontal AI builders **fail on the same two axes**: (a) they assume the customer can drive the AI, write copy, source photos, integrate payments; (b) none have SG-native integrations (PayNow, GrabPay, local delivery aggregator menu sync). Our agency wedge sells the *operator labor*, not the tool.

## B. Restaurant Vertical SaaS in Singapore

### Oddle
- **[Data]** White-label e-Shop for delivery/takeaway. **Zero start-up cost**, pay-per-booking on Oddle Reserve (reservations); commission/subscription model on e-shop side. Source: oddle.me/pricing. Tier 1.
- **[Data]** **Oddle Eats** = consumer-facing aggregator/directory bundled with merchant SaaS.
- **[Data]** Founded 2014, Singapore HQ, raised multiple rounds, regional presence.
- **Strength vs us:** Real restaurant infra (ordering, fulfillment, CRM, loyalty); not "just a website." Used by 1000+ SG restaurants.
- **Weakness vs us:** Microsite, not a full brand website (limited customization). Recurring fee model. They are arguably a partner more than a competitor — our site could embed Oddle's ordering widget.

### Chope
- **[Data]** Reservations + diner-facing marketplace. Seated **>100M diners** lifetime. Charges per-booking + fixed fees. Tier 2 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chope).
- **[Data]** Acquired by The Chope Group; pan-Asia footprint (SG, TH, HK, ID, etc.).
- **Strength:** Demand-side traffic (consumers find restaurants on Chope first).
- **Weakness vs us:** No real "website" — just a microsite on chope.co/restaurant-name. Restaurant doesn't own the relationship.

### Eats365
- **[Opinion/Assumption]** POS-led restaurant tech, includes some online-ordering microsite functionality. SG presence via eats365pos.com. Tier 3.
- **Weakness:** POS-first, website is afterthought.

### FoodPanda / Deliveroo / GrabFood (merchant pages)
- **[Data]** Merchant commissions **15–35%** of order value. FoodPanda raised consumer platform fee to SGD 0.60 from Jan 1 2025. Sources: Menuviel, Mothership, SingSaver. Tier 2.
- **Strength:** Provide a "page" + demand. Many SG independents treat their Foodpanda/Grab listing as their de-facto online presence.
- **Weakness vs owned website:** Restaurant rents the audience and pays 15–35% forever. Strong pitch angle for us.

**[Data]** **FoodMatch / FoodieSquare / GoFood:** Did not surface in 2025–2026 search; founder should verify whether still operating in SG or whether these are dated brand references. **Suggest founder verify via direct domain checks.** Gap.

## C. Local SG Web Agencies Targeting SMBs / F&B

### Verz Design
- **[Data]** **>4,000 projects, 180+ in-house staff**. PSG- and EDG-approved (up to 80% subsidy on EDG). Source: Verz Design / smartsinga.com / mediaplus reviews. Tier 2/3.
- **[Data]** SMB website pricing: not publicly listed but agency reviews cite SGD 3,000–10,000+ for SMB tier (general agency benchmark).
- **Strength:** Trust, scale, government-grant access. Established F&B portfolio.
- **Weakness:** Slow (custom design timeline weeks–months), not "AI-native."

### Subraa
- **[Data]** Freelance-led studio, "affordable" tier. Marketed pricing on Subraa.com for SMB packages. Has dedicated **Restaurant Website Design** landing page (https://www.subraa.com/restaurant-website-design-singapore/). Tier 3.
- **[Estimate]** Likely SGD 1,500–4,000/site based on freelancer positioning; founder should pull live pricing page.
- **Strength:** Closest direct price competitor to our SGD 500–1,500.
- **Weakness:** Solo capacity; no AI-velocity advantage.

### OOm
- **[Data]** Google Premier Partner, 30+ awards, SEO-led web design. PSG-approved. Tier 2.
- **Strength:** SEO baked in, brand trust.
- **Weakness:** Marketing-agency overhead; sites priced at agency tier.

### MediaPlus Digital
- **[Data]** Listed as PSG-approved web vendor. Publishes "Top 10 web design SG" content. Tier 3 (own marketing).
- **Strength:** Grant access.

### Orfeo Story
- **[Data]** PSG-approved eCommerce site builder. Tier 3.

### icreationslab / lemonade-it / alfdesigngroup / creativeans / singaporewebdesigner / konigle
- **[Data]** Range of SMB-targeting agencies surfaced in pricing-guide searches. Published tier ranges:
  - **Basic: SGD 1,500–5,000** (5–10 pages, responsive, template-driven)
  - **Standard: SGD 8,000–20,000** (10–15 pages, custom design)
  - **Premium: SGD 20,000–50,000** (fully bespoke)
  - Maintenance: SGD 150–300/month
- Sources: icreationslab.com, lemonade-it.com, alfdesigngroup.com, creativeans.com, konigle.com. Tier 3 (vendor blogs all converge on similar numbers — likely market consensus).

**[Opinion] Category takeaway:** **Our SGD 500–1,500 sits below the floor of the local agency Basic tier.** We're competing on price + speed + AI-native delivery. Once PSG is applied, however, Verz/OOm effective price to customer drops to SGD 750–1,500 too — neutralizing our price advantage for grant-eligible restaurants. **Strategic question:** become PSG-approved, or target the non-grant-eligible / impatient segment.

## D. AI Coding / Site Tools for the Founders to Deliver

(Pricing as of early 2026; verify before commit. All USD.)

| Tool | Price | Notes (T2 sources: Altar.io, dyad.sh, Lovable, UI Bakery, Technically) |
|---|---|---|
| **v0 (Vercel)** | Free w/ $5 credits; paid **$20/mo** | React/Next.js bias. Good for component-level generation. |
| **Lovable** | Free 5 cred/day (30/mo, public only); **Pro $25/mo** | Best-in-class full-app gen; private projects require Pro. |
| **Bolt.new** | **Pro $20/mo**, 10M tokens, Git + Supabase | Fast iteration; full-stack. |
| **Replit Agent** | Core **$20–25/mo + $25 usage credits**; heavy users **$100–300/mo** total | Effort-based pricing can balloon. |
| **Cursor** | **$20/mo** Pro; $40/mo Business | Editor-based; for founder's own work, not customer delivery. |
| **Webstudio AI** | Free + paid tiers (verify) | Visual builder, AI-assisted, exports clean code. |

**[Estimate]** For a 2-person agency delivering 10–20 sites/month: realistic combined tooling cost **~USD 100–300/month** ($1,200–3,600/year), assuming heavy use of one primary tool (Lovable or Bolt) + Cursor + occasional v0. **Gross margin on a SGD 1,000 site = SGD 950+ at full price**, easily SGD 500+ even after PSG reimbursement applies.

**[Opinion]** Cost structure of competitors: a local agency paying SGD 5–8k/month in salary per designer/dev to ship a site can't match this. The "AI-native" cost advantage is real.

## E. PSG-Approved Web Vendors (the subsidized competition)

| Vendor | Status | Effective price to customer post-PSG (50%) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Verz Design** | PSG + EDG approved | ~SGD 2,500–5,000 (from SGD 5–10k list) | smartsinga, verzdesign.com |
| **Orfeo Story** | PSG approved (e-comm) | ~SGD 2,500+ (from typical SGD 5k+) | orfeostory.com |
| **MediaPlus Digital** | PSG approved | Similar tier | mediaplus.com.sg |
| **OOm** | PSG approved | Similar tier | oom.com.sg |
| **MSWeb International** | PSG approved (e-comm) | Similar tier | mswebinternational.com |

**[Data]** Post-Budget-2026 enhancement: PSG support rises to **70%** from 1 April 2026 — effective cost to customer drops further (e.g., SGD 5k site → SGD 1,500 net to restaurant). This **directly compresses the price advantage of our SGD 1,500 unsubsidized offering**.

**Source:** GoBusiness Gov Assist portal (https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg/productivity-solutions-grant/all-psg-solutions/) — founder should pull the current PSG-approved list there. Tier 1.

**[Opinion]** PSG is the single most important strategic variable. Three options:
1. **Become PSG-approved** (application + pre-scoped solution submission; competitors did it, so it's doable but slow).
2. **Target non-eligible customers** (foreign-owned outlets, brand-new pre-revenue restaurants, ineligible chains, or anyone who doesn't want to wait for grant disbursement).
3. **Price aggressively below post-subsidy floor** (e.g., SGD 500 walk-out price ≈ what a PSG-subsidized SGD 3k vendor charges net) and win on speed + zero paperwork.
