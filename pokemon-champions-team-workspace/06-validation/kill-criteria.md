# Kill Criteria

**Phase:** 8 — Validation
**Project:** pokemon-champions-team-workspace
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Confidence:** Medium-High (criteria explicitly tied to Phase 7 financial model and Phase 8 experiments)

---

## Why Kill Criteria Exist

The single biggest failure mode for side projects is **sunk-cost continuation past the point where the data has spoken**. The founder will be emotionally invested by Month 3-4 — exactly when the project most needs an honest "stop or continue" check.

Each criterion is:
- **Specific** — measurable, no wiggle room
- **Time-bound** — tied to a milestone
- **Pre-committed** — written here, before emotional investment peaks

If a kill criterion fires, the founder commits in advance to either pivoting to a clearly defined alternative OR shutting down.

---

## Hard Kill Criteria

These are bright-line stops. If any fires, the founder must pause and seriously consider stopping or pivoting.

### KC1 — No advisor by Week 4

**Trigger:** No competitive-player advisor signed (formal or informal) by end of Week 4 of the project.

**Rationale:** Founder credibility gap is the #1 risk. If we can't recruit even one mid-tier ladder player or content contributor as advisor, Persona C/B will reject the product. Better to know now.

**Action if triggered:**
- Pause MVP build
- Run 5 more direct conversations with potential advisors
- If still no advisor by Week 6, seriously reconsider whether founder should partner with a co-founder who has the credibility, or pivot to a less credibility-dependent product (e.g., pure utility tool with minimal coaching framing)

### KC2 — Customer-discovery interviews invalidate the pain framing

**Trigger:** From E1 (15 customer-discovery interviews), <8/15 confirm tool fragmentation OR matchup uncertainty as a top-2 prep pain unprompted.

**Rationale:** If the pain isn't validated, the product is solving the wrong problem. Building further is sunk cost.

**Action if triggered:**
- Synthesize what the *actual* top pains were
- If pivot to a different pain is feasible, restart Phase 4 strategy
- If no actionable alternative emerges from interviews, stop

### KC3 — Closed beta activation below 30%

**Trigger:** From E8 (closed beta with 30-50 users), <30% of invitees save 2+ teams within 7 days of getting access.

**Rationale:** Activation is the leading indicator of conversion. Below 30% means the MVP onboarding is broken or the value is unclear. Either fixable, but a known gate.

**Action if triggered:**
- Diagnose: bad onboarding or bad core value? User interviews of beta non-activators
- Iterate onboarding for 2-4 weeks
- If activation still <30% after iteration, the product needs a fundamental rethink

### KC4 — Public launch month-1 conversion below 1%

**Trigger:** Free → Paid conversion <1% in the first 30 days post-public-launch (E9).

**Rationale:** Below half of Conservative scenario. Indicates either market-fit failure OR pricing failure OR onboarding failure.

**Action if triggered:**
- Pricing test: lower Pro to $9 for 4 weeks; measure conversion
- Onboarding intervention: 5-min onboarding flow audit + redesign
- If still <1% after both interventions, likely a fundamental product-market mismatch; consider pivot

### KC5 — Month 6 MRR below $400

**Trigger:** End of Month 6 (Nov 2026) MRR <$400.

**Rationale:** This is the Conservative scenario floor for Q2. Below this, the project is economically a loss and the trajectory toward Optimistic is mathematically impossible.

**Action if triggered:**
- Pause new product investment
- Run a "sunset analysis": what would it take to reach Base from here? If the answer is implausible (e.g., requires a tripling of acquisition that isn't in the playbook), wind down
- Open conversations with key paying users: would they buy a 1-year prepaid subscription to keep the product running? Is there a soft acquisition opportunity?

### KC6 — TPC C&D received

**Trigger:** Any communication from TPC legal, even informal, requesting changes or shutdown.

**Rationale:** This was the lowest-probability risk in Phase 3 (TPC has not C&D'd a companion tool in 15+ years). But if it happens, the brand is dead in current form.

**Action if triggered:**
- Engage IP/legal counsel immediately
- Comply with reasonable requests (rebrand, take-down of any flagged content)
- If shutdown is non-negotiable, wind down and write retrospective

### KC7 — TPC ships native team management with feature parity

**Trigger:** A Champions content patch ships native: (a) >5 saved team slots free, (b) version history, (c) any meaningful coaching or matchup helper.

**Rationale:** Erodes 30%+ of value prop. Workspace + version history alone is no longer differentiating.

**Action if triggered:**
- Quickly assess: do AI coaching + longitudinal memory + cross-format sync + creator-content integration still differentiate enough for paying users?
- If yes, reposition messaging away from "workspace" toward "coach + memory"
- If no, consider migrating product to Showdown-primary (where TPC's native tools don't apply) OR shutting down

### KC8 — Founder velocity drops below 12 hrs/week sustainably

**Trigger:** Founder logs <12 hrs/week of project work for 4 consecutive weeks.

**Rationale:** Below this threshold, the project doesn't progress meaningfully. Better to acknowledge and either restructure life around more time OR formally pause/sunset.

**Action if triggered:**
- Honest self-assessment: is this a temporary external constraint (job, family, health) or sustained loss of motivation?
- If temporary: pause publicly, set a return date
- If sustained: wind down or hand off to a more committed contributor

## Soft Kill Criteria (Pivot Indicators)

These don't require shutdown but should trigger major reassessment.

| Trigger | Indicator | Pivot direction |
|---|---|---|
| Casual market is the only WTP segment | >70% of paying users score as Persona E in survey | Pivot to broader free + creator-content marketplace; abandon Tournament Prep tier |
| All 3 top creator partnerships decline | No partnership term sheet by Week 8 | Rebuild GTM around content + tournament sponsorships; expect slower ramp |
| Manual journaling adoption <15% | Journal entries / Pro user / week | Replace journaling with quick-tag voting; restructure pattern synthesis |
| AI coach hallucination complaints exceed 10% of users | Public complaints + sample audits | Major coach architecture overhaul before further marketing |
| Off-season churn >30%/month in Q2 | Cohort retention curves | Restructure as seasonal product; cap ambition; align cost structure |
| Tournament Prep WTP <50% of Pro WTP | Conversion ratios over 90 days | Restructure tier — fold TP into Pro at $15-18; eliminate the high tier |

## Pivot vs. Stop Decision Framework

If a kill criterion fires AND a credible pivot path exists, pivot. Otherwise stop.

**Credible pivot characteristics:**
- The new direction is supported by data we already have (not speculation)
- The new direction uses substantial existing assets (code, audience, brand)
- The new direction is achievable within the founder's runway
- The new direction has WTP signals at least as strong as the original

**Examples of credible pivot directions:**
- Pivot from "VGC tournament-prep workspace" to "Showdown-only ladder companion" if Champions native ships
- Pivot from "AI coach product" to "AI coach API for content creators" if the consumer side fails
- Pivot from "B2C subscription" to "B2B tournament-organizer tools" if VGC TO market shows demand

**Stop characteristics:**
- No credible pivot path exists with current assets
- Personal opportunity cost exceeds projected lifetime upside
- Founder no longer wants to do this work

---

## Final Note

The point of kill criteria is **not pessimism**. It's protecting the founder's time and capital from sunk-cost continuation. Most projects that fail do so because they continued past the point the data was telling them to stop. Pre-commitment to these criteria is the cheapest insurance available.

Re-read this document at every monthly check-in. If a criterion fires and the founder feels the urge to "give it one more month," that's exactly the moment the criterion is doing its job.
