# GTM & Cold-Outreach Economics — AI-Native Website Agency for US Med Spas

Research date: 2026-05-11. Prefer 2025–2026 data. Labels: **[Data]** / **[Estimate]** / **[Assumption]** / **[Opinion]**.

Context: 2-person team, < USD 4k working capital, currently Singapore. Wedge = pre-build an AI-generated sample website for the prospect, then cold-outreach with it; full build USD 4,000–8,000 + optional monthly retainer. Target metros: Dallas–Fort Worth or Tampa/Orlando.

Market size for grounding: ~11,500 US med spas in 2025, growing ~14% CAGR; US med spa market ~USD 8–19B depending on source/scope (variance is real — different definitions). Florida ~7,700 med spas (Apr 2026): Miami ~3,100, Orlando ~830, Tampa ~390. Texas = 2nd-largest, fastest-growing state market; Dallas described as TX's most competitive metro. **[Data]** grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/medical-spa-market; americanmedspa.org/resources/med-spa-statistics; rentechdigital.com (FL list); medicalmarketingfirm.com/resources/blog/med-spa-marketing-texas-2026/. Source rating: market-size figures B- (analyst estimates, wide spread); location counts B (scraper-derived, directionally fine).

## 1. Cold email to US med spas / medical practices

**[Data]** B2B cold email reply rates have fallen: ~5% avg in 2025, ~3.4% in 2026; 5–10% is "solid," 10–15% "excellent," 15%+ best-in-class on tight ICP + strong offer. (instantly.ai/cold-email-benchmark-report-2026; reachoutly.com/cold-email/response-rate/; belkins.io/blog/cold-email-response-rates) — rating B+ (vendor-sourced but consistent across sources).

**[Data]** Healthcare is actually *good* for deliverability: ~94.7% inbox placement vs ~81% for SaaS — older, stable domains, less spam saturation. (thedigitalbloom.com/learn/b2b-email-deliverability-benchmarks-2025/) — rating B.

**[Data]** Legality: cold-emailing a doctor's office / med spa for B2B web services is legal in the US. HIPAA governs *patient* PHI, not physician/practice contact data — it does not restrict commercial outreach to providers. CAN-SPAM applies and requires: (1) physical mailing address in the email, (2) working 1-click unsubscribe, (3) accurate subject line, (4) honor opt-outs within 10 business days. No prior consent required. Fines up to ~$46.5k per email if you ignore the rules. (djholtlaw.com/can-spam-and-hipaa-compliance-for-healthcare-email-marketing/; sparkdbi.com/blogs/hcp-email-marketing-compliance-hipaa-can-spam-physician-outreach-2026) — rating A- (law-firm + compliance sources).

**[Opinion]** A BAA (Business Associate Agreement) is only needed if you *touch patient data*. A spec-site + marketing-site agency should never receive PHI — keep it that way and the HIPAA surface is ~zero.

Tooling **[Data/Opinion]**: Apollo or Clay for data + enrichment; Instantly or Smartlead for sending + inbox rotation. Realistic cost: ~$100–250/mo for sending infra + ~$50–150/mo for data, plus a handful of secondary domains (~$15/yr each) warmed for 2–4 weeks before sending. A 2-person team can sustainably send ~50–200/day; healthcare-compliance norms suggest capping ~50/day/inbox.

**[Estimate]** Med spa front offices use a public email (`info@`, `frontdesk@`) that gets a lot of patient/booking traffic — your B2B pitch competes with appointment requests. Expect reply rates at the *low* end of B2B (≈2–5%) unless you reach the owner directly. Verdict: **viable but not a silver bullet** — best as one leg of a multi-channel motion, and far better if the spec site is attached/linked in email #1.

## 2. Cold calling med spas

**[Data]** B2B cold calling 2025: ~18% of calls become a "high-quality lead" for top reps; ~15% call-to-meeting for the best; 6+ dials/contact lifts contact rate up to ~70%. (instantly.ai/blog/the-truth-about-b2b-cold-calling-in-2025-...) — rating B-.

**[Data/Opinion]** Med spas are a *phone-heavy* business — they answer the phone because patients book by phone. A web/marketing agency calling a med spa front desk will hit a receptionist gatekeeper; ask for the owner or office manager, call mid-morning or mid-afternoon (avoid open/close rush, avoid Mondays). The Get Map Leads "Google Maps cold calling" thesis — call businesses whose website weakness is *visible before you dial*, removing the core objection — maps perfectly onto "I built you a better site, here's the link." (getmapleads.io/blog/cold-outreach-strategy-web-agencies) — rating C+ (vendor blog, but logic is sound).

**[Opinion]** Timezone is the killer for a Singapore team (see operations.md): DFW business hours 9am–5pm CDT = ~10pm–6am Singapore. Cold calling is the *single hardest channel* to run from SG without flipping to a US sleep schedule. **[Estimate]** Realistic: 30–60 dials → 5–10 conversations → 0–1 booked demo per session. Use it as the *follow-up* to a sent spec site, not cold from zero.

## 3. LinkedIn outreach to med spa owners

**[Data]** LinkedIn 2025: avg reply 10–15%; healthcare ~9.25% reply; personalized connection note ~9.4% vs ~5.4% without; multi-channel (email+LI+call) can lift replies ~287% over single-channel; ~100 connection requests/week is the safe ceiling. (belkins.io/blog/linkedin-outreach-study; expandi.io/blog/state-of-li-outreach-h1-2025/; outreaches.ai/blog/cold-outreach-benchmarks) — rating B.

**[Opinion/Estimate]** Reachability is uneven. Physician/MD owners and multi-location operators *do* maintain LinkedIn profiles. Solo-practitioner NPs/RNs and "aesthetician → med spa owner" types are *thinner* on LinkedIn and live on Instagram instead. Expect to find ~40–60% of target owners on LinkedIn, of whom ~10% reply. Best fit for larger DFW/Orlando practices and small chains. Async-friendly for a SG team — no timezone problem.

## 4. Instagram DM

**[Data]** Cold DM is *explicitly against* Instagram ToS — scraping usernames + mass-DMing gets accounts restricted/banned; 10+ spam reports triggers review; safe ceiling ~20–30 cold DMs/day/account, spread out; "comment-to-DM" (recipient initiates) is the *only* sanctioned automated path. (creatorflow.so/blog/avoid-instagram-bans-dm-automation/; spurnow.com/en/blogs/instagram-dm-automation-rules) — rating B.

**[Opinion]** Med spas *are* Instagram-native and the practice's IG is often run by the owner or marketing person — so a *manual, personalized* DM ("saw your reels, your site doesn't do them justice, made you this →") can land. But it does not scale, risks burning the account, and you can't automate it safely. **[Estimate]** Treat IG DM as a *manual, high-effort, low-volume* channel (~10–20 thoughtful DMs/day) — good for warm-ish targets you've already engaged with, not a primary funnel. Reply rate plausibly higher than cold email *per message* (5–15%) but volume-capped.

## 5. Industry conferences

**[Data]** Medical Spa Show 2026 (AmSpa) — Apr 9–12, Wynn Las Vegas, 200+ exhibitors. Exhibitor booths reportedly ~$15k–$40k+ all-in. Attendee/non-exhibit registration is much cheaper (hundreds of dollars). AmSpa membership: Basic $395/yr, Plus $845/yr (includes vendor-affiliate discounts, legal summaries, State of the Industry report). (medicalspashow.com; americanmedspa.org/become-a-member) — rating B+ (org's own pages; booth pricing is an estimate range, C+).

Other relevant events **[Data/Estimate]**: The Aesthetic Show (Vegas, summer), AmSpa regional Boot Camps, IMCAS (Paris — skip, EU-focused), Vegas Cosmetic Surgery / AAFE, plant-based "Aesthetic Next." Med spa agencies *do* recruit at AmSpa and Aesthetic Show — it's where owners gather.

**[Opinion]** For a < $4k 2-person team, *exhibiting* is out of the question (one booth = 4–10x your entire capital). Realistic play: attend ONE event as a $300–800 attendee, work the floor and after-parties, get 20–40 owner conversations, follow up with spec sites. ROI: plausible if 1–2 deals come of it (covers cost 5–20x), but it's a *Year-1.5+* move once they have case studies, not a launch tactic. **[Assumption]** Travel from Singapore to Vegas ≈ $1.5k–2.5k flights + hotel — also prohibitive at current capital.

## 6. Referral / partner channels

Ranked by realism for a small unknown agency **[Opinion/Estimate]**:

1. **Med spa consultants / fractional COOs / "med spa coaches"** — *most realistic*. They advise dozens of practices on operations and marketing, and routinely refer vendors. Build relationships with 5–10 of them; offer a referral fee or white-label the spec-site build. Async-friendly.
2. **AmSpa vendor directory / partner listing** — low cost ($395–845 membership + possible affiliate listing fee), gets you in front of owners who actively search the directory. (americanmedspa.org/vendor-affiliates) — rating B.
3. **PMS / booking platforms (Boulevard, Aesthetix CRM, Mangomint, Zenoti, PatientNow/RxPhoto, Symplast)** — these have partner ecosystems; integrating with / referring to them is realistic medium-term.
4. **Med spa franchises / MSO groups** (Skin Pharm, Glo30, Radiance, etc.) — high-value but slow; one MSO deal = many locations; hard to crack as an unknown.
5. **Equipment vendors (Allergan/AbbVie Aesthetics, Cynosure-Lutronic, Cutera, Sciton, Candela)** — they *do* run partner/co-marketing programs and there's precedent (e.g., Spark Medical Marketing builds marketing for Cutera/Cynosure/Sciton/Allergan clients). Realistic only once you have a track record — these are enterprise relationships. (partners.sparkmedicalmarketing.com; americanmedspa.org/vendor-directory/) — rating B-.
6. **Accountants / lawyers serving med spas (e.g., ByrdAdatto, healthcare-focused CPAs)** — possible but low-volume; lawyers refer compliance, not web.

**[Opinion]** Best near-term partner bet = **consultants + AmSpa listing**. Equipment-vendor and franchise channels are the *scale* play but require credibility you won't have at launch.

## 7. The "spec-site" wedge in US B2B

**[Data]** From the SG analysis: DataLane reported ~40% close vs ~10% when outreach includes a "specific diagnosis"; FirstSales reported ~4x reply lift when a sample/asset is included. I could *not* re-confirm a med-spa-specific number in this round. US-general corroboration **[Data]**: web-design cold-outreach guides repeatedly cite that leading with concrete value (AI audit report, personalized 5-min video, custom mockup) materially lifts reply + close rates; personalized cold emails get ~21–26% higher response; fixed-price packages close ~40–50% more than vague custom quotes; agency cold-outreach typical win rate 1–5%, higher "when you're calling with an [audit/mockup] ready to send." (getmapleads.io/blog/cold-outreach-strategy-web-agencies; smartreach.io/blog/cold-email-templates-for-web-design-services/; alore.io/blog/web-design-cold-email; quickmail.com/cold-email/templates/web-design-templates) — rating C+ (agency/vendor blogs, no controlled study; directionally consistent).

**[Opinion]** The "show, don't tell" wedge is *more* powerful for med spas than for restaurants: med spa owners are visually obsessed (Instagram, before/afters, branding), and a slick AI-built site next to their dated WordPress is a visceral gut-punch. AI tooling (v0, Lovable, Framer AI, etc.) makes the per-prospect spec-site cost ≈ a few hours + minimal API spend — the unit economics of "build first, pitch second" finally work. **Highest-conviction part of the whole plan.**

## 8. CAC benchmarks & sales cycle

**[Data]** No clean "cost to acquire a med spa as a *web* client" figure exists publicly. Adjacent: med spas spend 8–12% of revenue on marketing (~$2.5k/mo for a $300k/yr spa); their *own* patient CPA ≈ $130; full-service agencies "make sense once a spa clears ~$50k/mo." (prospyrmed.com/blog/post/how-to-lower-cac-...; zoca.com/post/complete-med-spa-marketing-playbook-2025; growth99.com/blog/med-spa-marketing-budget-manual/) — rating B-.

**[Estimate]** Building a CAC bottoms-up for the spec-site model:
- ~1–3 hrs to build a spec site + research a prospect ≈ ~$50–150 of founder time.
- To land 1 client: need ~5–10 demos → ~50–150 spec sites sent → ~50–150 hrs work ≈ **$2.5k–7.5k of founder time per client** (mostly sweat, near-$0 cash), plus ~$100–300/mo allocated tooling. If you *don't* pre-build every prospect (only build for warm replies), labor CAC drops to **~$500–1.5k**.
- Cash CAC ≈ very low (< $300/client) given near-zero ad spend — the constraint is *time*, not money. Good fit for a cash-poor team.

**[Estimate]** Sales cycle: SMB web project, owner-decision, mid-4-figure ticket → typically **2–6 weeks** from first touch to signed (faster than enterprise; slower than impulse). Med spa owners are busy clinicians — expect ghosting and "after Q3" delays. The spec site compresses the cycle (decision is "do I like this?" not "should I get a website?").

## Confidence summary
- **High**: cold email is legal to send to med spas (HIPAA non-issue if no PHI); CAN-SPAM rules; benchmark reply rates (email ~3–5%, LinkedIn ~10%); the spec-site wedge is real and unusually well-suited to this vertical; cash CAC is low / time CAC is high.
- **Medium**: conference costs (booth pricing is a range); partner-channel realism ranking; med spa metro counts; sales-cycle length.
- **Low**: no med-spa-specific spec-site conversion data; no clean web-client CAC benchmark; IG DM reply-rate estimate is a guess.
