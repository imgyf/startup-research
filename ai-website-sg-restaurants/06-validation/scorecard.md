## Verdict

**CONDITIONAL PROCEED — Run the 30-Day Validation Sprint First**

This is a defensible, modest-margin **lifestyle business** if executed well — not a venture-scale opportunity in its current form. The research validates that the core mechanic (spec-site cold outreach) has external evidence behind it, but four critical numbers remain SG-unverified: walk-in close rate, cold-email lift in SG specifically, per-site compute cost at production volume, and PSG approval timeline. Each can be tested cheaply (<SGD 1k combined) within 30 days. Treat the next 30 days as a kill-or-confirm sprint; do not scale spend or commit founder full-time hours until at least 4 of the 5 validation gates pass (see `06-validation/kill-criteria.md`).

**What changed from the original plan:** Add a subscription maintenance tier (SGD 79/mo) to lift LTV; reframe the cold pitch from "AI-built website" to "recover SGD X/month from FoodPanda commissions + Google ranking"; apply for PSG vendor approval in parallel (not after launch); start with walk-ins, not cold email, until revenue funds compute spend.

**The honest answer to the founder's question** — "Is this company worth building?" — is: **yes, worth testing for 30 days at SGD 800. Not yet worth committing 6-12 months and SGD 50k to.** Run the validation sprint and decide on Day 30.

If venture-scale matters more than lifestyle viability, the better idea is likely Variation B (subscription-first model in this market) or Variation C (different vertical with more acute pain). Both are pre-validated by the research.

## Score-by-Score Honest Talk

**Why isn't it higher?** Three structural drags pull this score from a 7 to a 5.5:
1. **Latent pain.** Owners aren't searching for what we're selling. We have to wake them up first, which is expensive.
2. **PSG-compressed competition.** Government-subsidized competitors structurally match our unsubsidized price. Becoming PSG-approved is mandatory long-term, not optional.
3. **No durable advantage.** Once Wix Aria ships better automated outreach (6-18 months), the wedge collapses to "operator labor" — still a real service, but not unique.

**Why isn't it lower?** Two structural lifts:
1. **The wedge has data behind it.** This is rare for early-stage ideas. Most "novel sales angles" lack any external evidence.
2. **Cheap to validate.** SGD 800 + 30 days settles the question. Most startup ideas need SGD 20k+ and 90+ days to test their core hypothesis. That asymmetry is real value.
