**Project:** ai-website-sg-restaurants
**Date:** 2026-05-11

## Variation A: The Original — "Spec-Site Cold Outreach Agency"

**Pitch:** One-time SGD 500–1,500 restaurant website. Cold outreach hook = a fully built sample site delivered to the prospect's inbox.

**Exciting:**
- Clear, easy-to-explain wedge ("we already built yours, want to see?")
- Low capex; works at small scale immediately
- Strong cold-outreach demo value (most agencies show generic portfolios)

**Risky:**
- One-time pricing caps LTV → must sell forever to grow
- ACV/CAC math is brutal: at SGD 500-1,500 ACV with manual outreach, 1-2% reply×close rates mean ~50-200 touches per sale → caps revenue at founder hours
- The "AI spec site" wedge will be commoditized inside Wix/Squarespace within ~12 months
- Restaurants are notoriously slow payers and high-touch buyers

**Verdict:** Workable as a service-business floor, NOT venture scale. A founder making SGD 8-15k/month is plausible if the spec-site mechanic actually lifts conversion.

---

## Variation B: "Restaurant-in-a-Box" Subscription

**Pitch:** SGD 99-249/month per restaurant. Includes website + Google Business Profile management + monthly menu/promo updates + reviews monitoring + basic SEO. Sample site is still the cold-outreach hook, but the close is a subscription, not a one-time.

**Exciting:**
- LTV becomes 12-36 months × ARPU instead of one fee → 8-20x economic value per close vs. Variation A
- Recurring revenue makes paid acquisition viable later
- Restaurants don't have to "decide" about a website — they decide about delegating online presence (lower mental friction)
- Defends against Wix AI: even if Wix generates a site for free, it doesn't update the menu, respond to reviews, run promo campaigns, or fix things when they break

**Risky:**
- Harder cold close than a one-time payment (subscription = ongoing commitment)
- Operating cost per account is non-trivial — must templatize monthly delivery hard
- Need to actually deliver value monthly or churn kills the unit economics
- Two-person team capacity caps subscriber count (~50-150 accounts max without automation)

**Verdict:** Strictly better business than Variation A if the team can deliver the ongoing service efficiently. Same wedge, much better economics.

---

## Variation C: "Different Vertical First" — Beachhead Pivot

**Pitch:** Same model (AI spec sites + cold outreach), but target a less-saturated vertical for the first 50 customers. Candidates: TCM clinics, tuition centers, beauty/aesthetic clinics, dental practices, F&B suppliers, trades.

Why pivot away from restaurants — even temporarily — for the first beachhead:
- Restaurants are **the** most-targeted SMB segment for website agencies; competitive density is already high (Wix templates, Squarespace, Oddle, Chope, local agencies, Fiverr).
- Restaurants have the highest "I just use Instagram" objection rate.
- Restaurants are dominated by vertical SaaS that already provides a microsite (Chope/Oddle/Eats365), so the "no website" claim is half-true.
- Less-covered verticals (clinics, tuition centers, trades) have higher ACV ceilings (SGD 2-5k common) and lower competitive density.

**Exciting:**
- Higher win rate per touch in less-saturated verticals
- Higher ACV → math works better at small scale
- Restaurants come back as Phase 2 once unit economics are proven elsewhere

**Risky:**
- Founders may have specific restaurant insight/network that doesn't transfer
- Some verticals are regulated (medical, financial) → content requires more care
- Sales cycles can be longer in B2B services

**Verdict:** Worth seriously considering if the team has no specific restaurant edge. If they DO have restaurant connections (which we haven't established), restaurants stay.

## Recommended Refinement

**Combine B + restaurant focus** (with C as a fallback if restaurant conversion is poor after 50 outreaches):

> **AI-built restaurant websites with a managed-online-presence subscription.** Cold outreach delivers a pre-built spec site as a hook. Close is **SGD 800 setup + SGD 99-149/month** for hosting, menu updates, Google Business Profile management, and review monitoring. Restaurants stay locked in because nobody else on their team will do the monthly work.

This preserves the founder's core thesis (spec-site wedge + restaurants) while fixing the LTV problem and adding defensibility against AI commoditization. The one-time path is still possible as a "lite tier" offering for budget-constrained prospects.

The research phase will test whether this model holds up against real Singapore restaurant economics and competitor positioning.

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- None new beyond what intake already flagged.

**Yellow Flags:**
- All three variations assume the spec-site cold-outreach lift is real. If it's not, none of them work. This is the #1 thing to validate cheaply (Phase 8).
- The founder selected one-time pricing in intake. Suggesting subscription is a meaningful shift — needs founder buy-in before locking it in as the recommended path.
