## Honest Summary of Research Findings

**Question asked:** "Is it worth building a mobile app for frequent flyers to receive alerts for availability of flight bookings with airline miles?"

**Direct answer:** **As literally described, no.** As described, the product is already shipped at scale by ≥2 better-resourced incumbents, lacks a defensible wedge, and operates in a category facing active US litigation.

### What the data shows

1. **Market exists and is real.** Seats.aero is at $8M ARR bootstrapped with 500K MAU. point.me has $38.5M raised and a $90M valuation. The category has clearly graduated past "is this a real business?"
2. **The category has ~6+ funded/profitable competitors** with brand recognition, partnerships, and data-acquisition infrastructure built over 3–5 years.
3. **Mobile-first is no longer differentiation** — Seats.aero v 2026.1.2 (iOS 26 Live integration) and Roame's iOS app already ship native push alerts. This is the founder's primary stated thesis, and it is invalidated by current market state.
4. **The two largest distribution moats — Amex Membership Rewards and Bilt — are already partnered with point.me and Points Path respectively.** Chase and Capital One remain unclaimed but these are 9–18 month enterprise BD cycles.
5. **Active legal jeopardy.** Air Canada v. Seats.aero (D. Del. 23-1177) has been pending since October 2023, ExpertFlyer has been forced to lose access to 26 airlines' award/upgrade data, and Star Alliance has launched a coordinated anti-scraping offensive. A new US-based scraping startup walks into the same liability.
6. **Coverage gaps (Cathay, Singapore, EVA, Emirates, BA) are universal and not solvable by software** because airlines withhold inventory from third parties.

### What the founder probably *thinks* the gap is, vs. what the gap actually is

| Founder's stated thesis | Actual market state |
|---|---|
| "Frequent flyers want a mobile app for award alerts" | Seats.aero + Roame already provide this |
| "Push notifications are missing" | Both market leaders have push notifications |
| "It's a clean greenfield niche" | $50M+ raised across the category; mid-late maturity |
| (Implicit) "I can win with better UX" | Pricing is converged at $5–12/mo / $99–129/yr — no UX premium |

### Where there *is* still real opportunity (in this category)

- **Card-issuer B2B2C distribution** — Chase, Capital One, US Bank, or non-US issuers. Different business; requires BD experience, not mobile-app skills.
- **Travel-advisor B2B SaaS** — white-label award-search for the ~30K US travel agents who routinely book client award trips. Underserved.
- **Strategic-insight AI overlay** — "explain *why* this redemption is good and which transfer partner to use" — a genuine universal complaint.
- **Non-US power-user market** — Asia-Pacific (Singapore KrisFlyer, Cathay Asia Miles) and EU (Lufthansa Miles & More, Flying Blue) where US-centric tools under-index.
- **Concierge / done-for-you** — service-business, scales linearly, but viable.

None of these align with the founder's one-line description.

## Verdict

| Light | Choice | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 | **Red** for the literal idea as described | "Mobile app for award availability alerts" with no other wedge. The play is structurally late. |
| 🟡 | **Yellow** for an adjacent variation | If the founder pivots to (a) AI-strategic-insight overlay, (b) travel-advisor B2B, or (c) non-US/regional focus, the case is open. |
| 🟢 | **Green** only if the founder has a structural unfair advantage not stated | Pre-existing card-issuer relationship, an audience (newsletter/YouTube channel), proprietary data access, or existing concierge ops. |

## Recommended Next Decision

**Stop the literal version.** Continue planning *only* if the founder is willing to pivot toward one of the three open angles, OR has an unfair advantage they did not state in intake.

For the rest of this Fast Track pass we will continue to score the **literal version** so the founder has a clear evidence-based "no" with everything documented. If they want a re-run targeting a pivot, that is a separate session.
