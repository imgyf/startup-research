## Verdict

**The pivot is roughly a wash. It fixes Singapore's two real problems (low ACV, PSG subsidy distortion) but introduces two new problems of comparable weight (a saturated/mature agency market, and offshore-operational friction), and slightly *worsens* competitive advantage and founder-market fit.** Per-deal economics are genuinely better; the binding constraint shifts from "will the wedge convert at all" (SG) to "can a 2-person offshore team win deals against entrenched US agencies on a partial-US schedule" (US).

**Recommendation: do NOT commit to the pivot yet.** Run the ~$1k, ~30-day validation pilot in `validation-playbook.md` *before* forming a US LLC. The pilot's Experiment 2 (does the spec-site pitch beat a generic pitch in this SMMA-saturated vertical?) and Experiment 3 (will US med spas hire an unknown offshore micro-team?) are the deciding tests. If both pass, the pivot is worth pursuing — the higher ACV is real. If Experiment 2 fails, **stop pivot-shopping** — the honest conclusion is that "AI website agency" is a structurally weak category for this team, and the right move is a fresh brainstorm, not a third city.

**If forced to choose right now between the two without further data:** marginally prefer **staying with SG-restaurants** — not because it's better, but because the team has location/timezone/operational advantages there, the validation cost is lower, and the SG analysis already mapped a concrete 30-day path. The US pivot's higher ACV doesn't clearly beat the SG version's lower friction. *Best option of all: validate before deciding — the $1k/30-day pilot answers it.*

## The Honest Bigger Picture

Both ideas land at ~5/10. That's not a coincidence — it's the category. "AI builds a spec site, we cold-outreach with it, they pay for the real one" has:
- a **wedge that's a tactic, not a moat** (anyone with Lovable copies it the day it works);
- **AI tooling commoditizing** under it (Wix Aria, Squarespace Blueprint, Durable);
- an **inverse ACV-vs-openness relationship** — wherever websites are expensive (US verticals), the agency market is saturated; wherever it's open (SG, emerging markets), ACV is low or subsidies distort it.

If the team wants a >7/10 idea, the move isn't a better city — it's pairing the website build with something incumbents do *poorly and recurringly* (so it's a retained service, not a one-shot project), in a vertical that *isn't* SMMA-saturated. Candidates worth a fresh look (see README): private preschools / Montessori / K-12; senior living / assisted living; veterinary specialty hospitals; custom home builders / high-end remodelers; country clubs. All have high-ish budgets, dated web presence, recurring update needs, and far less agency saturation than med spas / dental / legal / home services.
