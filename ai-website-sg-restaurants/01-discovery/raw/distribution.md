# Distribution / GTM — Reaching SG Restaurant Owners on <SGD 5k

_2-person team, ACV SGD 500-1,500 one-time. Research compiled May 2026. Labels: **[Data]** / **[Estimate]** / **[Assumption]** / **[Opinion]**._

| Rank | Channel | Why | Notes |

## 1. Cold Email

**[Data] Benchmarks for B2B cold email, 2025-2026:**
- Average reply rate: **3-5%** (Instantly, Cleanlist, Martal — multiple sources converge)
- Average conversion to deal: **0.2%** raw, ~2% with strong targeting (Instantly.ai)
- Top-quartile senders: 15-25% reply (deeply personalized)
- "Personalized outreach based on restaurant intelligence led to 4x higher response rates" (FirstSales)
- "When a pitch included a specific diagnosis of a restaurant's online visibility, conversion was 40% vs. 10% generic" (DataLane)

**[Estimate] What to plan for with the pre-built-site hook:**
- Open rate: 40-55% (subject line: "[Restaurant Name] — built you a sample site, 60 seconds")
- Reply rate: **8-12%** (3-4x lift over baseline because of the embedded preview link)
- Booked-call rate: 2-4% of sent
- Close-to-paying rate from booked call: 25-35%
- **End-to-end: ~0.8-1.5% sent-to-paid**

**[Estimate] Unit economics at SGD 750 ACV:**
- 1,000 emails sent → ~10 paying customers → **SGD 7,500 revenue**
- Compute cost of 1,000 pre-built sites at S$3-5 each: **S$3,000-5,000** [warning: this dominates]
- Email tool + warm IPs (Instantly, Smartlead): ~S$200/mo
- Time: ~30-50 hours for QC + replies + closing
- **Contribution margin per 1,000 sent ≈ S$2,500-4,500** before payroll. Marginal.

**[Opinion]** Economics only work if (a) pre-built-site generation cost stays under **S$2 per prospect** through caching, templated theming, smart batching; or (b) reply rate beats the 8% estimate. Both are achievable but neither is given. **Track cost/lead religiously from day 1.**

**[Data] Deliverability gotchas (SG-specific):**
- Singapore restaurants often use `@gmail.com`, `@yahoo.com.sg`, `@hotmail.com` as their public address — these have aggressive spam filters
- Many use `info@theirrestaurant.com.sg` on shared hosting → mid-tier filtering
- "Bounce rate must stay <2%" / "Spam complaint <0.1%" — verify every address with Hunter / NeverBounce before send
- Use SPF + DKIM + DMARC; warm up sending domain for 2-3 weeks; cap at 30-50/day per inbox
- Use a **secondary domain** (e.g. `tryaisite.sg`) not your primary

**[Data] PDPA-compliance for cold B2B email** — see customers.md §6. Safe if: target is business address, content is business-relevant, unsubscribe present, sender identified.

**[Recommended tooling]** Instantly.ai or Smartlead (multi-inbox rotation) + Apollo/Hunter for emails + a custom scraper for restaurant URL → menu screenshot → AI-generated sample site pipeline.

## 2. Cold Walk-ins

**[Data] Why walk-ins beat email for this segment:**
- "The restaurant industry is notoriously difficult to penetrate because owners are busy running their businesses, and getting past the front desk or finding direct contact information is nearly impossible." (FirstSales)
- F&B owner is **physically present** in their venue 6-7 days/week — no other B2B vertical has this property at this scale.
- Pitch is visual (tablet showing their pre-built site) — collapses 3 emails into 90 seconds.

**[Estimate] Throughput:**
- 8 visits/day per person × 5 days = 40 visits/week per salesperson
- Visit time including travel: 30-45 min in dense areas (Tanjong Pagar, Bugis, Joo Chiat, Tiong Bahru, Holland Village)
- Hit-rate to "owner is in and willing to look at tablet": **40-60%** (lunch off-peak / 2-4pm is best)
- Of those who look: book demo/close on the spot **15-25%**
- **End-to-end close from 40 weekly visits: 3-6 customers/week per salesperson**

**[Estimate] Unit economics at SGD 750 ACV:**
- 40 visits × 4 weeks × 5% close = **8 customers / month / person** → **SGD 6,000 revenue / person / month**
- Cost: transport (~S$200/mo), tablet + LTE (~S$80/mo), pre-built site compute for ~160 prospects (~S$500)
- **Contribution per person ≈ S$5,200/mo** — viable
- Two people scaling this = **S$10k+ MRR within 90 days** if execution is clean

**[Opinion]** This is the single best channel for the first 50 customers. It is also the most painful and ego-bruising. Founders must do this themselves. Do not hire SDRs for it.

**[Assumption]** Need a **cluster strategy** — work one neighborhood per day, 10-20 restaurants in walking distance. Tanjong Pagar lunch district, Joo Chiat / Katong, Tiong Bahru, Telok Ayer, Bugis, Holland Village, Dempsey, Jalan Besar, Chinatown.

**[Data Gap]** No SG-specific walk-in conversion data found. Estimates above triangulated from US/EU vertical SaaS-to-restaurant playbooks.

## 3. F&B Industry Groups

**[Data] Restaurant Association of Singapore (RAS):**
- Membership: "S$235.40 for establishments with <S$2M revenue" (Ordinary Member tier) (RAS website)
- "Represents 500+ members, 800 brands, 5,000+ outlets" (RAS)
- Application: 7-10 working days
- Public members listing: https://ras.org.sg/membership/members-listing/

**[Opinion]** RAS membership at <S$250 is a cheap entry. But:
- You probably can't join as a non-F&B vendor (Ordinary membership is for F&B operators). Look for **Associate / Affiliate** tier or **event sponsorship** routes.
- Real value is the **directory + events** — sponsoring 1 RAS networking event (estimate **S$2,000-5,000**) puts you in front of dozens of owners. Better than 100 cold emails.
- Also relevant: **Singapore Food Manufacturers' Association (SFMA)**, **Federation of Merchants' Associations Singapore (FMAS)**, **SBF F&B chapter**.

**[Estimate]** ROI: probably 2-5 customers per event sponsored if you can demo on-site. Cost-per-acquired ~S$500-1,500. Acceptable.

**[Action]** Try the RAS directory first as a scraping/contact source for the cold-email/walk-in flow before committing to membership fees.

## 4. POS / Reservation / Payments Partnerships

**[Data] Relevant SG-active platforms:**
- **Oddle** (SG-founded, online ordering + reservations) — clear partner program candidate
- **Chope** (reservations, SG HQ) — has partners/affiliates page (https://www.chope.co/singapore-restaurants/pages/partners)
- **StoreHub** (POS, mostly MY/TH/PH but some SG) — has Partners-for-Growth program
- **Stripe** (no SG-specific F&B partner channel publicly)
- **Xero** (accounting; not restaurant-specific but huge SMB reach) — has app marketplace + partner directory
- **Sevenrooms**, **TablesReady**, **GoFood (Grab)** — possible
- **DBS / OCBC / UOB SME banking** — sometimes do SMB partner ecosystems

**[Opinion]** Highest-leverage partnership: **Oddle**. They sell ordering+reservations but explicitly do not sell websites. A "we build your site, your Oddle integration goes in by default" handshake creates mutual referral. Same with **Chope** for reservations. **Approach within first 60 days.**

**[Estimate]** Realistic outcome: 1-2 referral deals per month per partner once activated. Long ramp (3-6 months to first referral). Don't bet first revenue on it; bet month 6 scale on it.

**[Assumption]** Need a one-page partner deck and a referral-fee structure (10-20% rev share is conventional) to even open these conversations.

## 5. Cold LinkedIn / DM

**[Data] LinkedIn benchmarks 2025:**
- Connection accept rate: 27%
- Reply rate after connection: 11%
- Cold response rate overall: 10.3% (2x cold email) — "India, Australia, Singapore show 10-12% replies" (Outreaches; Belkins 2025)
- Personalization beyond first name: +340% reply (Belkins)

**[Opinion] Caveat for F&B:** Independent restaurant owners in SG are **massively under-indexed on LinkedIn**. They live on WhatsApp + Facebook Messenger + Instagram DM. LinkedIn works for:
- Multi-outlet group owners / F&B group founders (Les Amis, Jumbo, Crystal Jade, Paradise Group, 1855 F&B, etc.)
- Hotel restaurant GMs
- Cloud kitchen operators
- Newer concept-driven restaurants founded by younger operators

**[Estimate]** Useful for 10-15% of the addressable market. Skip for v1 unless explicitly targeting multi-outlet.

**[Better alternative for the same persona]** Instagram DM. Owners read their own IG inbox. Reply rates on warm, well-researched IG DMs to SG F&B can be **15-25%** [Estimate]. Risk: IG flags accounts for spammy DMs above ~30-50/day. Use a real, populated business account.

## 6. Hawker / Coffeeshop Dialect

**[Data]** "Many elderly, independent hawkers lack the skills or the capital to create an online presence" (Cambridge / ASEAN Briefing). IMDA's **Hawkers Go Digital** focuses on e-payments + ordering, not websites.

**[Opinion] Skip for v1.** Buyer is fundamentally different:
- Different price ceiling (likely <S$200)
- Different feature set (just a Google Business Profile + simple landing page; not a "site")
- Different sales motion (NEA, HDB, community development councils, dialect-speaking field reps)
- Different funding rails (Hawkers Go Digital, not PSG)
- Different language (English-light, dialect/Mandarin/Malay-Tamil)

**[Assumption]** Worth revisiting in 12-18 months as a separate SKU (e.g., "Hawker Profile, S$99") via NEA/IMDA channel partner route, once core restaurant ICP is locked.

## 7. Content / SEO ("restaurant website Singapore")

**[Data]** "SEO Singapore" and related restaurant-website terms are saturated with established players: OOm, Verz Design, Hashmeta, Ninjapromo, ZestReach, Senthil. (Multiple SEO agency listicles, 2025-2026)

**[Opinion]** Ranking organically for "restaurant website Singapore" inside 12 months is **unrealistic** for a 2-person team. SEO is a **defensive moat**, not an acquisition channel for year 1.

**[Action]**
- Buy your `.sg` domain + run thin Google Business Profile + 1 landing page
- Write 2-3 "show, don't tell" case studies as customers ship (with permission)
- Skip the agency listicle / link-bait route; defer to year 2
- **Cheap experiment:** Google Ads on "restaurant website builder Singapore" — bid is probably S$3-8 CPC. Budget S$500 trial. Watch CAC, kill if >S$200.

## Cost summary for first 90 days (2-person team, under SGD 5k)

| Line | Cost (SGD) | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Domain + Google Workspace x2 | 200 | |
| Cold email tool (Instantly, 2 seats) | 300 | 3 months |
| Email-finder (Hunter / Apollo low tier) | 200 | |
| Pre-built sample site compute (~1500 sites @ S$3) | 4,500 | **Dominant cost** |
| Tablet (used) + LTE plan | 350 | For walk-ins |
| Transport for walk-ins (3 months) | 600 | |
| RAS event sponsorship / membership test | 500 | optional, can skip |
| Reserve | 350 | |
| **Total** | **~7,000** | over budget — must cut |

**[Opinion]** SGD 5k is **tight but feasible** if compute-per-site is engineered down to S$1-2 (template reuse, batch generation) and walk-ins handle the first 20 customers (no email spend month 1). Plan:
- Month 1: pure walk-ins, ~S$1,000 spend, target 12-20 customers
- Month 2: layer in cold email with revenue funding it, ~S$2,000 spend
- Month 3: partnerships + double down on whichever channel is working
