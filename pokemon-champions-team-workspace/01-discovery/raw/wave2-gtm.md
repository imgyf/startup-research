### Geographic skew

- **Strongest English market:** US (anchor for both YouTube + IRL Regionals).

## 2. Past Creator-Tool Collaborations

### Inside Pokémon

- **WolfeyVGC's Patreon already behaves like a tool subscription** — tiers gate "tournament prep battling," "metagame breakdowns," "early access to tournament teams," "team reviews." The **10,893 paid patrons paying ~$5–$25/mo** is a direct, unambiguous WTP signal for paid VGC prep. A tool that complements (not competes with) this layer is the right wedge.
- **Pokémon GO Hub × Pokebattler** — formal partnership where Pokebattler tech powers PoGoHub's Battledex. Pokebattler runs an explicit **Influencer Partnerships program**: free Pokebox memberships in exchange for influencer mentions; Pokebattler reciprocates by sharing partner channels. This is a **template for mid-tier outreach**.
- **PvPoke ↔ Pokebattler** — cross-tool integration with revenue sharing (Pokebattler subs sponsor PvPoke). Pattern: smaller tool integrates with bigger tool, gets discoverability + a kickback.
- **Pikalytics** — operates more as a public good than a sponsorship hub; cited everywhere but minimal commercial overlay. Treat as **data-layer / co-marketing partner**, not paid sponsor channel.

### Adjacent communities (proven models)

- **Mobalytics × LCS** (League of Legends): partnered with the official league to power amateur-tier player profiles. **Lesson:** if Play! Pokémon ever opens an official partner slot for a tool, that's the platinum tier — but for now, target unofficial.
- **Mobalytics streamer/team partnerships** + GINX content deal (Dec 2020). Drove path to **7M+ users / $11.25M raise**. **Pattern:** prosumer tool + creator co-branded content series.
- **Chess.com × GothamChess (Levy Rozman) × Hikaru Nakamura**: Levy commentates Chess.com events (PogChamps, Titled Tuesdays, Candidates). Chess.com built the audience pipeline by **letting creators own the narrative and providing the stage** — Chess.com captures sign-ups via in-stream events. **Lesson:** sponsor / power tournaments, don't try to compete on broadcast.

**Synthesis:** the right model is **(a) Patreon-tier-locked tool access** for top creators (Wolfey style) **+ (b) Pokebattler-style influencer program** for the long tail **+ (c) Chess.com-style tournament sponsorship** for trust building.

## 5. Geographic Prioritization

- **Tier 1 (launch):** US, Canada, UK, Ireland, Australia, NZ — English-speaking VGC audience aligned with WolfeyVGC's audience and the regionals circuit.
- **Tier 2 (low-cost expansion):** EU English + Spanish — partner with Victory Road (already bilingual, owns Latam coverage).
- **Tier 3 (high-value, high-friction):** Japan — best players, but content insularity; would need a Japanese-language site + a JP partner. Defer until Series A.
- **Tier 4 (opportunistic):** Korea — Sejun Park goodwill, small but real scene.
- **Verdict:** English-only at launch. Spanish localization in Phase 2 (low marginal cost, high LATAM upside via Victory Road).

## 7. First-90-Days Launch Playbook

### Days 0–14 — Foundation
1. **Lock Wolfey deal first.** Time-sensitive: his Patreon is growing 5K+/month and he is the single highest-leverage relationship. Offer: free Pro for his $25 Patreon tier members, revshare on direct sign-ups, advisor equity option. Aim to ship a co-branded feature within 30 days.
2. **Reach Cybertron in parallel** with onboarding-flow co-branding.
3. **Apply to Victory Road resources page** + propose tournament-report sponsorship.
4. **Open a Pikalytics conversation** on data licensing / co-credit. Decide partner-or-overtake by day 30.
5. **Launch SEO programmatic spine:** matchup pages, EV-spread pages, archetype pages (rain/sun/TR/sand/HO), regulation explainers. Target the **AI coach + matchup calculator** keywords where SERP density is lowest.

### Days 15–45 — Trust building
6. **Smogon Discord + Showdown chat rooms:** *participate first, do not link-drop.* Run AMAs only after 2+ weeks of helpful contribution. Treat their advertising rule (60-day penalty) as a hard line.
7. **r/stunfisk (257K)** strategy: post helpful analyses (not "check out our tool"), launch with a community-first offering (e.g., "free matchup calc, no signup"). Pre-commit to mod outreach first.
8. **Own r/PokemonChampions early** — moderate share, pin community resources, become a trusted account before the sub fills with low-effort posts.
9. **Twitch caster outreach:** offer free Pro accounts to all named casters (Joe Brown, Aaron Zheng, Sierra Dawn, Rosemary Kelley, Yuki Zaninovich) ahead of Indianapolis Regionals (May 29–31).

### Days 46–90 — Tournament-driven traction
10. **NAIC (June 12–14)** is the inflection moment — by then we need: signed Wolfey deal, Victory Road listing, working co-stream-friendly product mode (live damage calc / matchup HUD), and at least one viral SEO landing page ranking.
11. **Twitch Rivals adjacency:** sponsor a Limitless community tournament ($500–$2K prize) on Champions to plant a "presented by" tag — cheaper than official TPCi sponsorship, similar perceived legitimacy.
12. **Worlds (Aug 28–30)** = the launch crescendo. Goal: in-product "Worlds prep mode" co-marketed with at least 2 of the top-5 creators above; a paid social burst on X/TikTok timed to Worlds week.

**Primary acquisition channel = creator-led YouTube partnerships (Wolfey + Cybertron tier).** Reddit and SEO are amplifiers, not primary engines. Twitch rides on the YouTube relationships.
