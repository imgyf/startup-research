# Kill Criteria — US Med Spa Pivot

**Phase:** Pivot validation
**Project:** ai-website-us-medspas
**Date:** 2026-05-11

---

Pre-commit these with your co-founder *before* spending a dollar on a US LLC. The pivot is attractive on paper (5× ACV, no subsidy headwind) precisely the way Singapore was attractive on paper — and the SG analysis found the headline number hid structural problems. Same discipline applies here.

---

## K1 — Metro SAM is too thin
**Trigger:** Experiment 0 desk audit finds <40 independent (non-franchise, non-PE-portfolio) med spas with a poor/dated/no website across Tampa + Orlando combined.
**Means:** The warm-lead pool can't sustain a 200+-prospect campaign; you'd burn through the metro in weeks.
**Action:** Re-run the audit on Scottsdale/Phoenix + Nashville + Charlotte. If the combined four-metro warm SAM is still <120, the per-metro economics of this vertical don't support a focused offshore push — drop the pivot or rethink the target vertical.

## K2 — Delivery is too slow or too low-quality
**Trigger:** Experiment 1 — median >2.5 hr per spec site at small batch, OR non-founder quality rating <6/10, OR <70% of sites "look more premium than the spa's current site."
**Means:** The "build first, pitch second" volume math doesn't close — you can't produce enough good spec sites to feed the funnel.
**Action:** Don't kill yet — invest a week in a reusable med-spa component library + tighter prompts, then re-test. If still >2 hr/site, the model only works build-on-warm-reply (much lower volume) — recompute whether that's worth a US LLC.

## K3 — The wedge is theatrical in this vertical
**Trigger:** Experiment 2 — Cohort C (spec-site pitch) reply rate is NOT ≥2× Cohort A (generic) AND ≥1.3× Cohort B (diagnostic-only). I.e., building the actual site adds little over just pointing out flaws.
**Means:** In a SMMA-saturated vertical where free demos are standard, the spec site isn't a differentiator — you're just another agency pitch.
**Action:** **Drop the pivot.** This is the load-bearing premise. If it fails here *and* the SG version was only CONDITIONAL, stop pivot-shopping for a better city — go back to brainstorming a category that isn't "another website agency." (See README "If both ideas are weak" section.)

## K4 — Cold outreach can't reach med spa owners at all
**Trigger:** Experiment 2 — all cohorts <1% reply rate (sent-to-reply).
**Means:** Med spa owners are too pitch-fatigued / their inboxes too saturated to reach cold.
**Action:** Test owner-direct channels (LinkedIn to MD owners, manual IG DM, consultant referrals) before concluding. If owner-direct also <1%, this vertical is unreachable for a cold-outreach motion — pivot vertical or channel.

## K5 — The offshore-trust discount is fatal
**Trigger:** Experiment 3 — "trust: you're offshore" / "trust: tiny unknown team" accounts for >50% of objection weight, AND zero clients sign knowing you're a Singapore 2-person shop.
**Means:** US med spas won't hand $5–7k to an unknown foreign micro-team without a US-based presence.
**Action:** Don't kill the *idea*, but recognize it now needs a US face — a US-based contractor running sales calls, a US co-founder, or relocation. All are big lifts on <$4k. Either commit to building that, or pivot to a **white-label** model: sell spec sites *to* US agencies/consultants who put their name on it (lower margin, no trust problem, but you become a subcontractor).

## K6 — Operational/tax friction outweighs the ACV upside
**Trigger:** Experiment 4 — the accountant says the ECI/tax treatment for this exact setup is messy or materially taxed, OR you cannot get a US business bank account (Wise/Relay/Mercury all decline), OR the EIN/entity timeline pushes past ~10 weeks.
**Means:** The operational tax of being offshore eats the higher US margins.
**Action:** Reconsider against alternatives — notably **Australia** (Gold Coast / Sydney / Melbourne med spas: English, AUD strong, far less SMMA saturation than the US, dense enough for occasional in-person, ~3h timezone offset from SG instead of 13h, no US-entity hassle). Run a one-week mini-audit of the Australian med spa web market before committing either way.

## K7 — Founder can't sustain the partial-US schedule
**Trigger:** During the 30-day pilot, either founder reports the 8pm–2am SG sales-call window is unsustainable, OR demo-call no-show/reschedule rate is so high (because you can only offer awkward hours) that it kneecaps conversion.
**Means:** The timezone tax is a real product/operations problem, not just an inconvenience.
**Action:** Either commit to one founder permanently flipping to a US-overlap schedule (and price/scope accordingly), or treat the US market as async-only (email + LinkedIn + recorded Looms, no live calls — harder to close $5–7k deals), or pivot to Australia/UK for a friendlier overlap.

---

## Continue Criteria — Green Light to Form the LLC and Scale

- ✅ Tampa+Orlando warm SAM ≥80 independent spas with a poor/dated/no site (Exp 0)
- ✅ Spec sites: ≤90 min each, ≤$3 compute, ≥7.5/10 quality, ≥80% "more premium than current" (Exp 1)
- ✅ Cohort C reply rate ≥2× generic and ≥1.3× diagnostic-only; ≥3 demos; ≥1 signed in 6 weeks (Exp 2)
- ✅ Offshore-trust objection <30% of weight; ≥1 client signs knowing you're offshore (Exp 3)
- ✅ Accountant confirms clean ~$300–600/yr setup; Wise/Relay pre-qualifies; entity timeline ≤8 weeks (Exp 4)
- ✅ Both founders willing to run a partial-US schedule for 6–12 months without burnout (Exp 7 / observed)

Hit 5 of 6 → form the Wyoming LLC and scale. Hit 3–4 → fix the gap and re-test the failing one. Hit ≤2 → drop the pivot; the US version isn't clearly better than the SG version, and neither clears the bar for committing.

---

## The Honest Meta-Kill

If both the SG-restaurants idea (scored 5.5, CONDITIONAL) **and** this US-medspas pivot fail their respective core-wedge tests, the conclusion is not "try a third city." It's: **"AI website agency" as a category is structurally weak for a 2-person bootstrap** — the wedge is a copyable tactic, AI tooling is commoditizing, and ACV and market-openness are inversely correlated. At that point the right move is a fresh brainstorm (use the office-hours / brainstorming skill), not another pivot. Don't let two months of sunk research talk you into a third.
