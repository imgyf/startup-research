# Intake Brief

**Phase:** 1 — Intake
**Project:** ai-website-sg-restaurants
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Confidence:** High (founder-stated facts)

---

## The Idea

An "AI-Native" website-building agency for SMBs in Singapore, starting with **restaurants** as the beachhead. Differentiator: instead of pitching "we can build you a website," cold outreach includes a **pre-built spec site** generated by AI tooling — prospects see their actual website before being asked to pay. If they like it, they pay the full amount and the team finalizes/ships the site.

Core thesis: AI compresses spec-site production cost to near-zero, so "show first, sell second" becomes economically viable at scale.

## The Founders

- Small team with co-founder (2+ people)
- Mix of capabilities across the team
- Under SGD 5,000 working capital — needs to monetize within weeks
- Implicit: bootstrap, lean, scrappy

## The Market

- **Geography:** Singapore (beachhead)
- **Segment:** SMB restaurants without a "nice" website
- **Estimated population:** ~4,000-7,000 F&B establishments in Singapore (to verify in research)
- **Currently solve via:** Wix/Squarespace/Shopify DIY, Facebook/Instagram-only presence, vertical SaaS (Oddle, Chope, Eats365 microsites), small local agencies, or no website at all

## The Business

- **Pricing thesis:** SGD 500–1,500 one-time per website (volume play)
- **Revenue model:** One-time project fee (TBD: hosting/maintenance retainer?)
- **Sales motion:** Cold outreach (email/DM/walk-in) with pre-generated spec site as the hook
- **Unit economics target (implied):** Spec site cost < 5% of price; conversion > a few % of outreach

## Constraints & Preferences

- **Hard budget cap:** SGD 5k means no paid ads, no SDR hires — manual outreach only
- **Speed requirement:** Spec site generation must be cheap and fast (minutes, not days) for the model to work
- **No regulatory exposure** (websites are unregulated in SG for general business)
- **PDPA considerations** if collecting prospect data for cold outreach (Singapore's data privacy law)

## The "Unfair Advantage"

Selected: **Pre-built spec sites drive higher conversion than traditional cold-pitching**.

This is the load-bearing assumption of the entire business. If spec sites do NOT meaningfully lift conversion vs. a normal portfolio + cold pitch, the model collapses — at SGD 500-1,500 ACV, the founders can't afford a high CPL.

## What "Worth Building" Hinges On

Three questions the research and validation must answer:

1. **Does the spec-site wedge actually convert better?** And by how much vs. a portfolio screenshot or generic mockup?
2. **Can a 2-person team profitably produce spec sites + finished sites at SGD 500-1,500 in Singapore?** Including their own time at any reasonable hourly rate.
3. **Is the Singapore restaurant SMB market large enough and reachable enough** to sustain a meaningful business at this price point?

## Hard Questions (asked, founder to answer over the research)

- Why are you the right team? Domain? Restaurant industry connections?
- If Wix launches "AI generates your restaurant site from your Instagram" tomorrow (they're close), what's your moat?
- Strongest argument against: AI-generated websites are commoditizing fast (V0, Lovable, Bolt, Wix AI, Squarespace AI). The differentiator may erode in <12 months.
- Have you talked to 5+ restaurant owners? What did they actually say (not what you hoped)?

---

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- ACV of SGD 500-1,500 with budget under SGD 5k creates a math problem: cold outreach typically needs 50-200+ touches per close in SMB. At zero ad spend, this is pure manual labor — capping revenue at the founders' personal sales capacity.
- AI website tools (Wix AI, Squarespace AI, Durable, 10Web, Hocoos) are launching restaurant-specific templates. The "AI generates a sample for you" feature is already being commoditized inside the tools themselves.

**Yellow Flags:**
- "AI-Native" is a positioning claim, not a product. Customers don't buy "AI-Native" — they buy outcomes (more reservations, online orders, found on Google).
- Restaurant owners are notoriously time-poor and skeptical of cold outreach in Singapore. The spec site is meant to fix this, but conversion lift is unproven.
- One-time pricing limits LTV. Most successful SMB website businesses are subscription/retainer (Wix, Squarespace, Shopify).
