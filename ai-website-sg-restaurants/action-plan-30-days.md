### Day 29-30 (Final review)

  1. Read every entry in `assumptions-tracker.md` and update status

## What If Something Goes Off-Plan?

| If… | Then… |
|-----|-------|
| Week 1 spec-site pipeline doesn't work | Stop walk-ins. Spend Week 2 on tooling. Resume walk-ins Week 3 with delayed timeline. |
| Week 1 walk-ins all empty (owners not present) | Test different time windows (10am–noon or 4-6pm); recalibrate cluster strategy |
| First customer asks for ordering checkout (out-of-scope) | Stick to the pitch: link to FoodPanda/Oddle; offer ordering as SGD 800 add-on |
| Cohort B reply rate identical to Cohort A | Don't kill email yet — re-test with stronger personalization (rich preview thumbnail, diagnostic line in subject); rerun 50 |
| Both co-founders feel overwhelmed by Day 14 | Reduce to 2 walk-in days/week each, focus on closing demoed prospects, accept slower ramp |
| Bank balance hits SGD 1k before Day 30 | Pause discretionary spend, prioritize closing booked demos, eat into reserve only for must-have tools |
| PSG response says "no AI deliverables" | Don't kill — adapt pitch to manual-customization framing ("we use AI to draft, then customize each site by hand for SG context"). Re-apply. |

## Why This Plan Works

It's **cheap** (SGD 950), **time-boxed** (30 days), and **decision-forcing** (Day 30 decision with pre-committed kill criteria). It tests the load-bearing assumptions in the right sequence: compute economics → walk-in viability → objection landscape → cold-email lift. It produces real revenue evidence, not just opinion or survey data. And it respects the most important constraint: **the founder's question wasn't "is this perfect?" it was "is this worth building?" A 30-day kill-or-confirm test is the most honest answer.**
