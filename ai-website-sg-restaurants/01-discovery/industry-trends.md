# Industry Trends

**Phase:** 3 — Research (cross-cutting synthesis)
**Project:** ai-website-sg-restaurants
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Confidence:** Medium-High (most trends sourced to Tier 1/2; some opinions clearly marked)

---

## Trend 1: AI Website Generation Is Commoditizing — Fast

- **Wix Harmony / Aria** (agent-driven site building), **Squarespace Blueprint**, **Durable** (30-second name→site), **10Web Restaurant Builder**, **Framer AI**, **Hocoos**, **Lovable**, **v0**, **Bolt.new** — all 2024-2025 entrants. Prices USD 7-25/month [Data, Tier 1-2]
- **The "AI generates a site for you" feature is no longer a differentiator** — it's table stakes. The differentiator is now *who does the operator labor* (research, content, photos, customization, hand-off).
- **[Opinion] Window for "AI-native" as a marketing claim: 6-12 months.** After that, the term is meaningless in market positioning. Move the claim from "AI-native" to outcomes ("ranked on Google by Friday").

## Trend 2: Singapore F&B Operator Pressure Is Increasing

- **307 outlet closures/month in 2025** vs 254/month in 2024 [Data, Tier 2]
- **82% never recorded a profit; >60% don't survive 5 years** [Data, blog Tier 3 — needs triangulation, but widely cited]
- **Margins 3-8% typical** [Data, Tier 2/3]
- Drivers: high rent, food costs, manpower shortage, declining dine-out frequency in 2025
- **Implication:** Operators are simultaneously **more receptive** to ROI-positive tools and **less able to pay upfront cash**. Subscription/PSG-subsidized pricing fits the moment.

## Trend 3: Singapore Government Is Actively Subsidizing F&B Digitalization

- **PSG enhanced to 70% support** from 1 April 2026 (was 50%), SGD 30k annual cap per UEN [Data, EnterpriseSG Budget 2026, Tier 1]
- **Refreshed Food Services Industry Digital Plan (IDP)** released July 2025 — explicitly includes **AI use cases for F&B** as a pillar [Data, IMDA, Tier 1]
- **CTO-as-a-Service** advisory and **SMEs Go Digital** programs actively channeling SG restaurants toward digital vendors [Data, IMDA, Tier 1]
- **October 2025: EnterpriseSG launched three new initiatives** to help F&B manage costs and accelerate digital adoption [Data, Tier 1]
- **Implication:** Government tailwind is real but conditional on **vendor pre-approval**. Becoming PSG-approved is the single highest-leverage strategic move available to this startup.

## Trend 4: Owned Digital Channels Are Underused vs. Aggregators

- **25.1% of F&B sales** are online (Oct 2025) — but mostly through **FoodPanda / Grab / Deliveroo at 15-35% commission** [Data, Tier 1-2]
- **78% of diners search online** before visiting [Data, Tier 2]
- **63% of restaurant web visits are mobile** [Data, Tier 2]
- **[Opinion]** Many SG independents are functionally renting their entire customer relationship from aggregators. Owned websites are the obvious arbitrage — but only if the owner can quantify the savings. The sales pitch must do this math for them.

## Trend 5: Cold Outreach With "Shown" Personalization Beats Generic Pitches 3-4x

- **40% close rate** vs 10% for restaurant pitches with specific online-visibility diagnosis [Data, DataLane, Tier 2]
- **4x higher response rates** with personalized restaurant intelligence [Data, FirstSales, Tier 2]
- **+340% reply lift** from personalization beyond first name [Data, Belkins 2025, Tier 2]
- **[Opinion]** The thesis of this entire startup is anchored on this evidence — and the evidence is real, but US-based. **The single most important pre-launch validation is to replicate the lift in SG: 50 cold walk-ins + 200 cold emails with vs without pre-built sites.**

## Trend 6: Restaurant Tech Spend Is Growing — But "Marketing Software" Lags

- **Restaurant tech spending priorities (2025):** marketing software (30%), reservation software (30%), staff scheduling (26%) [Data, FSR Magazine 2025, Tier 2]
- "Reliability (37%), ease of use (33%), price/affordability (32%)" are the top evaluation criteria [Data, FSR 2025, Tier 2]
- **Implication:** Websites/SEO sit in "marketing software" — the most contested 30%. Most owners are buying *something* in this category already; we're competing for budget already allocated to a competitor (often FoodPanda fees treated as marketing spend).

## Trend 7: Singapore SME AI Adoption Tripled in One Year

- **SME AI adoption: 4.2% → 14.5%** in 2024 [Data, IMDA, Tier 1]
- 84% of AI-using SMEs use off-the-shelf GenAI [Data, IMDA, Tier 1]
- **Implication:** Culture is ready for AI-delivered services. "AI built your site" is not scary in 2026 SG. But the buyer's emotional anchor remains "show me the result," not "tell me about the tech."

## Trend 8: Singapore Cold-Email Regulation Is Lenient for B2B

- B2B cold email is legal under PDPA + Spam Control Act with: relevant content, unsubscribe option, sender identity, business-domain targeting [Data, multiple Tier 2 sources, 2025-2026]
- DNC registry does **not** apply to business landlines or `info@` email addresses
- **No surfaced PDPC enforcement actions** targeting B2B cold senders in 2025-2026
- **Implication:** Cold email is a viable channel without legal risk if executed cleanly. Volume should still stay reasonable (<100/day per sending inbox) to avoid deliverability issues.

---

## Strategic Implications

1. **Move fast** — the AI-native window is 6-12 months before Wix Aria does what we're doing on autopilot.
2. **Apply for PSG approval in parallel to operating** — it's the single biggest economic lever and competitors already have it.
3. **Reframe the pitch** from "AI-built website" (a feature) to "recover SGD X/month in lost reservations + FoodPanda margin" (an outcome).
4. **Embrace one-time pricing** (matches high F&B mortality) but add a low-friction monthly maintenance tier (SGD 50-100) so the wedge doesn't have to be the entire business.
5. **Be honest about the wedge being temporary** — the long-term moat is operator labor, local trust, and SG-native integrations (PayNow, Oddle, Chope), not the AI itself.

---

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- **AI commoditization risk:** if Wix Aria ships "cold-outreach with auto-generated samples for prospects" in 2026-2027, the entire wedge collapses. Position is defensible only via local operator labor, not technology.

**Yellow Flags:**
- **PSG approval timeline is non-trivial** — competitors (Verz, OOm) took months/years. If application takes 6+ months, the first 6 months must operate unsubsidized.
- **One-time-pricing weakness against subscription competitors:** Oddle, Chope build LTV through ongoing fees. If a restaurant pays SGD 1,500 once and never again, we're a project-services business, not a SaaS-shaped company. Founders should be clear-eyed about this.
