## Strategic Rule of the GTM

> **Tournament calendar drives the cadence. Creator partnerships drive the conversion. Reddit organic + SEO drive the long tail.**

Everything below is sequenced against the VGC 2026-2027 tournament calendar.

## Tournament Calendar — The GTM Clock

| Window | Demand level | Our action |
|---|---|---|
| **May 2026** (now) | Building (post-launch land grab) | MVP build sprint |
| **May 29-31** Indianapolis Regional | Demand spike | Closed beta test ("regional prep partner" angle) |
| **June 12-14** NAIC | High demand | Public beta launch IF ready; otherwise more closed testing |
| **Late June - July** Multiple Regionals | Sustained demand | Public launch + creator content burst |
| **August 28-30** Worlds 2026 | **Peak annual demand** | Worlds-prep mode launch; Tournament Prep tier feature push |
| **Sept-Nov** Off-season | Demand trough | Cold-start risk; lean on creator content + product polish |

**Critical inference:** The window between **public launch (target: late June 2026)** and **Worlds 2026 (Aug 28-30)** is ~8-10 weeks — that's our highest-leverage window of the year. Anything that slips past Worlds drops into the off-season trough.

## SEO Strategy

**Avoid:**
- "Pokémon team builder" (Showdown moat)
- "Pokémon Champions AI coach" (VGCCoach already #1)
- Generic "Pokémon strategy" (Smogon moat)

**Pursue (long-tail):**
- "VGC prep notebook"
- "VGC tournament prep tool"
- "Pokémon Champions matchup [archetype]" (matchup-specific landing pages)
- "Reg G [archetype] counter" (recurring per-format content)
- "How to climb Pokémon Champions ranked"
- "Best Pokémon Champions team for [tournament]" (event-specific content)

**Format:**
- Structured archetype prep pages built programmatically from Smogon usage data + curated counter notes — generate 20-50 pages on launch, expand monthly
- Each page links to sample teams users can import to their workspace
- "How [creator] prepped for [tournament]" — case studies (with creator partnership)

**Cadence:**
- 5-10 new prep pages per month
- Updates triggered by usage-stat shifts and tournament results

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- None.

**Yellow Flags:**
- The GTM is creator-dependent. If all three top creators decline, we lose the highest-leverage acquisition channel and need a much slower content-and-organic path.
- Reddit posts can fail unpredictably. We need at least 2-3 different framings tested.
- The off-season demand trough (Sept-Nov) is genuinely lethal for retention if we don't ship Tournament Prep tier features in time for Q1 2027 spike.
