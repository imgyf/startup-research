# Kill Criteria

**Phase:** 8 — Validation
**Project:** ai-website-sg-restaurants
**Date:** 2026-05-11

---

These are **pre-committed** conditions under which the founders agree to stop or pivot. They exist specifically to protect against sunk-cost thinking — by the time you hit one, you'll have spent enough emotional energy that you'll be tempted to push past it. The whole point of writing them now, before the data comes in, is to take that decision away from you mid-stream.

**Reaffirm these with the co-founder before starting validation. Sign-off (literal or symbolic) is non-negotiable.**

---

## Kill Criteria (Stop or Pivot Required)

### K1 — Wedge fails the look test
**Trigger:** In Experiment 2 (20 walk-ins), <40% of owners are willing to look at the tablet when shown a pre-built spec site of their own restaurant.

**What it means:** The fundamental wedge — "people will give 60 seconds to see their own site" — doesn't work in SG. The entire model rests on this and it's broken.

**Action:** STOP the restaurant beachhead. Re-run the same experiment in 1 other vertical (e.g., clinics or salons — 20 walk-ins) to test if it's restaurants specifically or the wedge generally. If second vertical also <40%, **abandon the spec-site model entirely** and consider a different startup.

---

### K2 — No paying customers in 30 days
**Trigger:** End of day 30, 0 paying customers across all channels.

**What it means:** Even with the spec-site wedge, the SG restaurant SMB is not converting at any rate within reach of the model.

**Action:** STOP and conduct a structured post-mortem with all data from Experiments 1-4. Three branches:
1. If Experiment 1 (compute) succeeded but Experiment 2 (walk-ins) closed 0: **wrong sales motion or pitch** — try a different vertical or sales motion (e.g., Instagram DM at higher volume)
2. If Experiment 1 failed: **wrong tooling stack** — re-evaluate AI vendors and retry in 60 days
3. If both succeeded but no close: **wrong product** — survey 30 prospects on what they would buy, redesign offer

---

### K3 — Compute cost overrun
**Trigger:** Per-site cost in Experiment 1 exceeds SGD 8 (4× target) at small batch.

**What it means:** Cold-email economics are broken. The model can still work walk-in-only, but the addressable scale is capped.

**Action:** **Don't kill, but pivot:** drop cold-email channel for v1, focus walk-in motion, raise pricing to SGD 1,800 minimum (margin requires it). Revisit compute optimization in month 2 after first 10 closes.

---

### K4 — Founder burnout / co-founder rift
**Trigger:** Either co-founder reports inability to continue walk-ins (mental or physical), OR co-founders cannot agree on next action after 2 consecutive Sunday reviews.

**What it means:** Team risk has become an execution risk. Even a working model fails without the team.

**Action:** STOP walk-ins for 1 week. Talk it through. Either:
1. Recommit with clear new role split, OR
2. One founder takes 2-month break (other operates solo), OR
3. Dissolve the partnership cleanly and document learnings

Bootstrap businesses with 2 founders die more often from co-founder conflict than from market problems. **Take the warning signs seriously.**

---

### K5 — Spec-site lift is theatrical, not real
**Trigger:** In Experiment 4 (cold email A/B with 50 each cohort), Cohort B (pre-built site link) reply rate is NOT at least 2× Cohort A (generic pitch) reply rate.

**What it means:** The visual demo doesn't add value in the asynchronous channel. The whole "AI-spec-site at scale" premise depends on this lift being real outside of face-to-face.

**Action:** **Don't kill — narrow scope:** the business is now walk-in-only. Re-run financial model with walk-ins capping at ~SGD 10k MRR/founder. If that's acceptable to both founders as a lifestyle business, continue. If not, pivot to Variation C (different vertical with more vocal pain).

---

### K6 — Pain is too latent
**Trigger:** In Experiment 3 (no-sale interviews), >50% of objections fall into "I don't see why I need this" / "Already on Instagram, doing fine" category.

**What it means:** Restaurant owners don't recognize the loss. The diagnostic pitch and spec site aren't doing enough work. The market is too unconsciously incompetent for cold-outreach economics.

**Action:** Two paths:
1. **Iterate the pitch:** Add a side-by-side Google search comparison ("here's how you show up vs. your top competitor — they get the click"). Re-run 10 walk-ins. If still >50% don't-see-need, pivot.
2. **Pivot vertical:** Targets with more vocal pain — clinics (compliance pressure), tuition centers (parent-perception pressure), beauty (visual brand pressure).

---

### K7 — Cash runs out
**Trigger:** Bank balance drops below SGD 500 with month-2 revenue forecast under SGD 4k.

**What it means:** Runway exhausted. The bootstrap math doesn't work.

**Action:** STOP all marketing spend immediately. Either:
1. Both founders take parallel contract work (SGD 50-80/hr) to refund the runway, OR
2. Pause the venture for 3 months and revisit, OR
3. Accept the experiment failed and close down cleanly. Refund any prepaid customers' remaining balance.

---

## Continue Criteria (Green Light to Scale)

The converse: if all of these hit by end of month 1, scale aggressively in months 2-3.

- ✅ ≥3 paying customers from 20 walk-ins (15% close rate)
- ✅ Per-site compute cost ≤ SGD 3 at batch
- ✅ Experiment 4 (cold email) replicates ≥2× cohort lift
- ✅ Both co-founders willing to sustain pace through month 2-3
- ✅ At least 1 maintenance-tier signup among first 5 customers
- ✅ EnterpriseSG response on PSG indicates approvability within 12 months

---

## Decision-Day Protocol

End of day 30: book a 2-hour block in calendar. No customer work. Just the two founders, the data, and this document.

Walk through each Kill Criteria and each Continue Criteria. **Decide.** Write the decision down. Commit a one-pager to the project repo documenting:
- What did we learn?
- Which kill / continue criteria triggered?
- Decision: SCALE / PIVOT (to X) / STOP
- If SCALE: month 2 plan
- If PIVOT: new hypothesis + new validation plan
- If STOP: what we'd do differently next time

---

## Why This File Exists

Founders who don't pre-commit kill criteria almost always push past them. They've invested too much emotionally to stop. The result is months of zombie operation that ends in resentment and personal cost. **The cost of a clean kill at day 30 is one month and SGD 5k. The cost of pushing through a broken model for another 6 months is hundreds of hours, savings depletion, and the friendship.**

If this seems pessimistic, remember: the kill criteria are *thresholds*, not predictions. The base case (per Phase 7 projections) says the business clears all of them comfortably.
