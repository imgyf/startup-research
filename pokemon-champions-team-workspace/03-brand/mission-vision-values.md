## Mission

*Why we exist — present-tense, action-oriented.*

Three drafts:

1. **(A)** *We help Pokémon competitive players turn one-off teambuilding into a season-long workflow that compounds — so the prep you do this week strengthens you next month, not just tomorrow's match.*

2. **(B)** *We give serious Pokémon players a workspace that remembers — so the lessons you earn never disappear between matches, between teams, or between tournaments.*

3. **(C, recommended)** ***We help VGC players prep with rigor — without losing what they've learned along the way.***

Why C wins: shortest, names the audience cleanly (VGC players, not "Pokémon players"), names the failure mode it solves (losing what they've learned), and signals our pace ("rigor" not "magic").

---

## Vision

*The world we're building toward — aspirational but credible.*

Three drafts:

1. **(A)** *Every serious Pokémon player has a notebook that gets smarter with them — the prep substrate of competitive Pokémon, the way Strava is for runners and Lichess is for chess players.*

2. **(B)** *Competitive Pokémon prep is no longer a fragmented stack of tools held together with duct tape — it's a single workspace that compounds across seasons and grows with the meta.*

3. **(C, recommended)** ***Competitive Pokémon prep becomes a craft — measurable, learnable, and shared — instead of a memory-and-hope routine.***

Why C wins: it names what we want to change (memory-and-hope → measurable craft), and the language ("craft," "shared") sets us apart from "AI does it for you" hype.

## Values

Five values, each with a behavioral definition. Values are decision tools, not slogans.

### 1. Grounded over magical

We never ship AI that bluffs. Every claim cites a simulator run, a damage calculation, or a stats source. If we don't know, we say so. We'd rather a feature feel modest and trustworthy than feel impressive and wrong.

> *Decision test:* If a feature would feel "wow" but might hallucinate a moveset, we don't ship it.

### 2. Built with players, for players

Persona C and Persona B are advisors, not abstractions. We launch with at least one named competitive player visible on the team. We post drafts on Reddit before locking decisions. We credit users whose feedback shaped features.

> *Decision test:* Have we asked a real Persona C player about this in the last 30 days?

### 3. Workflow over wow

We optimize for the recurring weekly grind of tournament prep, not for first-impression demos. Quiet features that compound matter more than bright features that fade.

> *Decision test:* Does this make next month's tournament prep easier than this month's?

### 4. Earn the term "competitive"

We say no to features and copy that water down for the casual market. Free tier exists, but it serves the funnel — never the brand. Tournament Prep is the strongest tier and we are unapologetic about that.

> *Decision test:* Would Persona C respect us for shipping this? Would Persona B stop trusting us if we did?

### 5. Respect the community

Showdown, Smogon, Pikalytics, Poképaste, and the creator economy built this scene. We integrate, credit, and partner — we don't pretend to be standalone. We follow each community's posting rules. We don't link-drop in Smogon Discord.

> *Decision test:* Have we credited the open-source projects, content creators, or community members whose work this depends on?

## What These Values Mean in Practice

A few concrete examples of how the values would resolve real product decisions:

| Decision | Resolved by value |
|---|---|
| "Should we add a quick-start preset for non-VGC casual play?" | **Value 4** (earn the term "competitive"). If it pulls product toward casual, no. If it's a free-tier inbound funnel only, scoped narrowly, ok. |
| "The AI is 80% accurate — ship it?" | **Value 1** (grounded over magical). Not until citations + groundedness are enforced. 80% on competitive Pokémon = wrong on 20% of high-stakes decisions = brand risk. |
| "We could announce a partnership with [creator] before deal closes." | **Value 5** (respect the community). No. Wait until it's truthful. |
| "A user submits a bug report — should we mention them in the changelog?" | **Value 2** (built with players). Yes, with permission. |
| "A feature would take 3x effort to ship right but could ship cheap-and-dirty." | **Value 3** (workflow over wow). If it lasts a season and compounds, take the 3x. If it's a first-impression demo that fades, don't ship it cheap. |

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- None.

**Yellow Flags:**
- The "Champion's Notebook" name uses "Champion" — close enough to "Champions" (the game) to risk perceived conflation with TPC. Domain + trademark check is mandatory before lock-in. Alternatives ranked above.
- Values are easy to write and hard to keep. Phase 6 product reviews and Phase 8 validation should explicitly test value adherence (e.g., audit AI hallucination rate against Value 1 each month).
