# Pivot Brief — From SG Restaurants to US Med Spas

**Phase:** Pivot intake
**Project:** ai-website-us-medspas
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Confidence:** High (founder-stated context + prior project carryover)

---

## What This Is

A **focused validation pass** on a proposed pivot of the AI-website-agency model. The original project (`../ai-website-sg-restaurants/`) scored **5.5/10 — CONDITIONAL**. Two structural drags hurt it: (1) low ACV — SGD 500-1,500 per restaurant website; (2) Singapore's Productivity Solutions Grant rose to 70% in April 2026, letting subsidized competitors match the unsubsidized price.

The pivot keeps the **same model** — pre-build an AI-generated spec website for a named prospect, cold-outreach showing it, full build on conversion — but changes **geography and vertical**: from **Singapore restaurants** to **US med spas / aesthetic clinics** (target metros: Dallas–Fort Worth or Tampa/Orlando).

## Why This Pivot Was Proposed

US med spa websites sell for **USD 4,000–8,000** (vs ~USD 1,100 in SG), with **no government subsidy distorting pricing**, and the spec-site wedge should land harder on visually-obsessed buyers (before/afters, Instagram aesthetic).

## What Carries Over From the SG Analysis (Not Re-Derived)

- **The model mechanics** — spec-site cold outreach, AI delivery via Lovable/v0/Bolt/Cursor, ~80%+ gross margin on the build, walk-in-vs-async channel trade-offs.
- **The wedge evidence** — DataLane (40% close vs 10% with a "specific diagnosis"), FirstSales (4× reply lift). US-general B2B corroboration found but no med-spa-specific controlled data.
- **The AI-commoditization clock** — Wix Harmony/Aria, Squarespace Blueprint, Durable etc. closing the gap; ~6-18 month window on the "AI-native" claim.
- **The honest read on the unfair advantage** — there isn't a durable one; the wedge is a tactic, not a moat.

## Founder Context (Unchanged)

- Small team with co-founder (2 people)
- Under SGD 5k working capital (~USD 3.7k)
- Currently based in Singapore
- Bootstrap, scrappy, need to monetize within weeks

## What This Pivot Validation Must Answer

1. **Is the US med spa web/marketing agency market saturated** — and if so, can a 2-person unknown survive in it?
2. **Does the spec-site wedge survive** in a vertical where free demos/mockups are already standard practice?
3. **Is the per-metro independent market large enough** — DFW vs Tampa/Orlando?
4. **Can a Singapore-based 2-person team operationally pull this off** — US entity, tax, banking, timezone?
5. **Do the better unit economics ($5-7k ACV) outweigh the new problems** (saturation, operational friction)?

## Decision Frame

The output is a comparison: **is `ai-website-us-medspas` clearly better than `ai-website-sg-restaurants`, roughly the same, or worse?** And if neither is compelling, what's the honest alternative?

---

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- The pivot may simply trade one set of structural problems for another of similar magnitude — fixing ACV and subsidy distortion while introducing market saturation and offshore-operational friction. That would make it a lateral move, not a breakthrough.

**Yellow Flags:**
- Founders have no US presence, no med spa / healthcare / aesthetics background, and a punishing timezone offset. Founder-market fit may be *weaker* than the SG version.
- "Med spas" is the canonical SMMA-agency target vertical — owner inboxes/DMs may already be saturated with web/marketing pitches.
