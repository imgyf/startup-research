# Research Gate — Go/No-Go Checkpoint

**Phase:** 3.5 — Research Gate
**Project:** ai-website-sg-restaurants
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Verdict:** **YELLOW LIGHT — CONDITIONAL PROCEED with required modifications**

---

## What the Research Found (60-Second Summary)

### Positive signals
- The **spec-site cold-outreach wedge is real and data-supported**: 40% close rate vs 10% generic; 4x reply lift [Tier 2 data, US-based].
- **Market size is adequate**: ~27-30k addressable SG outlets, ~10-18k beachhead (independents without own websites).
- **Pricing is below competitive floor** (SGD 500-1,500 vs local-agency basic tier SGD 1,500-5,000).
- **AI delivery cost-structure works**: ~80% gross margin even at SGD 1,000 ACV.
- **Walk-ins are uniquely viable** for F&B because owners are physically on site daily.
- **Singapore is leaning into F&B + AI digitalization** — government tailwind via IDP, CTO-as-a-Service, EnterpriseSG.

### Negative signals
- **PSG enhancement to 70% (April 2026) compresses competing agencies to SGD 1,500-2,500 effective** — exactly our list price, with stronger trust and government endorsement.
- **AI website tooling is commoditizing fast** — 6-12 month window for the "AI-native" marketing claim.
- **One-time pricing at SGD 500-1,500 creates a treadmill problem**: must sell forever to grow; founder hours cap revenue at ~SGD 8-15k/month.
- **Customer pain is latent, not vocal** — owners aren't asking for websites, so reverse-prospecting on "show your loss" is required.
- **High customer mortality** (60%+ in 5 years, 82% never profit) makes any retention/LTV hard.

### Mixed signals
- **Pain is acknowledged but not prioritized** by owners. Marketing software is in their tech-spend top 3, but they default-allocate it to FoodPanda commissions rather than owned sites.
- **SG-specific cold-outreach lift is unverified** — all numbers triangulated from US/EU studies. Must be pilot-tested before scaling.

## Verdict: Yellow Light

This idea is **worth building** — but **not exactly as described**. The founder's original framing has a structural pricing problem that the research surfaces clearly.

### Required modifications to the original plan

1. **Apply for PSG vendor approval in parallel with launch** — not after. This is the single highest-leverage strategic move and competitors have a 1-3 year head start.

2. **Reframe the pitch from "AI-built website" to "recover SGD X/month from FoodPanda commissions + lost Google reservations."** The AI-native angle is rapidly commoditizing.

3. **Add a SGD 50-100/month maintenance tier** to amortize CAC. Pure one-time at SGD 500-1,500 means the business is bounded by founder selling capacity forever.

4. **Walk-ins first, not cold email.** First 20-30 customers come through neighborhood walk-ins. Cold email comes online in month 2-3 once revenue funds compute spend.

5. **Engineering a per-site cost <SGD 2 is required before scaling email.** Without this, cold-email economics break.

### What Must Be True for "GO"

These are the gating questions the validation phase will answer in the next 30 days:

| Gate | Threshold |
|------|-----------|
| Spec-site lift in SG | 50 walk-ins → ≥5 paying customers (10% close) within 6 weeks |
| Per-site compute cost | <SGD 2/site at batch volume |
| Pricing acceptance | Owners pay SGD 1,000-1,500 unsubsidized without grant inquiry stalling >50% of deals |
| Co-founder durability | Both co-founders complete 100 walk-ins in 30 days without burnout |
| PSG path | EnterpriseSG response indicates ≤6-month approval feasibility |

**If 4 of 5 gates pass:** GO (full investment).
**If 2-3 gates pass:** Pivot — likely toward Variation B (subscription) or Variation C (different vertical).
**If 0-1 gates pass:** STOP. Better idea exists.

### Pivot Options the Research Surfaced

If the original concept fails the gates, three pivots are pre-validated by the research:

1. **B-tier: "Restaurant-in-a-Box" subscription** — SGD 800 setup + SGD 99-149/mo. Adds defensibility against Wix Aria and raises LTV from SGD 1k to SGD 4-7k.
2. **C-tier: Different vertical beachhead** — clinics, tuition centers, beauty/dental. Higher ACV (SGD 2-5k), less competition.
3. **D-tier: PSG-approved pivot** — pause direct sales for 3-6 months, focus on becoming PSG-approved with a SGD 3,000 list (SGD 900 net) and target the grant-driven inbound funnel.

## Founder Decision Point

**Recommendation:** Proceed with a 30-day validation sprint (see `06-validation/`). Treat the next month as a kill-or-confirm test. The cost is one month of founder time + ~SGD 800 spend. The value is a real-data answer to "is this worth building" — far better than another 2 months of strategy thinking.

The data says the wedge is plausible, the market is reachable, and the economics can work — **conditional on field-validation of three numbers**: SG-specific reply/walk-in close rate, per-site compute cost, and PSG eligibility timing.

If the founder is unwilling to do 100 walk-ins in 30 days, this is the wrong startup for them regardless of how good the data looks — the model relies on operator effort, not capital.

---

## Flags

**Red Flags:**
- PSG-eligible competitors at 70% subsidy structurally match or beat the unsubsidized price wedge.

**Yellow Flags:**
- AI commoditization risk: 6-12 month window.
- Latent-pain market means harder cold close than the spec-site lift data suggests.
- One-time pricing caps growth.
- Three load-bearing numbers (lift, compute cost, PSG eligibility) all need primary validation.
